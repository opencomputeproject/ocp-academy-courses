The final module brings the course back to practical application.

E S S compliance is layered. Product standards, installation codes, fire codes, international standards, local authorities, and insurers all shape what a project can actually deploy.

N F P A 855, N F P A 70, N F P A 70E, I C C and I F C codes, U L 9540, U L 9540A, C S A C800, I E C 62933, and FM Global guidance each play different roles. None of them should be treated as the whole answer by itself.

The O C P Ready E S S matrix is useful because it turns the requirements into concrete planning categories: suppression, segregation, detection, ventilation, and technology selection.

The deployment workflow is the bridge. Define the use case, classify the architecture, identify hazards, map standards, engage reviewers, build evidence, plan operations, and contribute back to the community.

That is how the white paper becomes a working tool.
