A practical E S S workflow starts before equipment selection.

First, define the use case. Is the system supporting rack ride-through, row buffering, facility backup, power smoothing, grid interaction, or microgrid operation?

Second, classify the deployment. Identify location, coupling, distribution, controls, energy or power orientation, hybridization, and grid interaction.

Third, identify the storage technology and credible hazards. Include electrical, thermal, gas, explosion, chemical, fire propagation, access, and emergency response considerations.

Fourth, map the codes and standards. Include N F P A, I C C, U L, C S A, I E C, regional rules, local amendments, and insurance guidance.

Fifth, engage the authority having jurisdiction, insurer, fire protection engineer, operations team, and first responders early.

Sixth, build the evidence package. That may include product certification, U L 9540A or C S A C800 testing, hazard mitigation analysis, drawings, separation plans, ventilation strategy, suppression strategy, emergency procedures, and maintenance plans.

Finally, contribute lessons back to the O C P Energy Storage Systems project. The subgroup exists so the community can keep improving practical guidance.
