Insurance guidance can be decisive in real projects.

The white paper references FM Global data sheets because loss-prevention requirements often influence what owners can deploy, where they can deploy it, and what protection measures are acceptable.

Three FM Global references are especially relevant. Data Sheet 5-28 addresses DC battery systems. Data Sheet 5-32 addresses data centers and related facilities. Data Sheet 5-33 addresses lithium-ion battery energy storage systems.

These documents can influence location, separation, fire-rated construction, detection, ventilation, suppression, and loss-prevention expectations. The Summit speakers emphasized that insurance requirements may appear late if they are not brought into the process early.

That late discovery is expensive. It can force redesign, relocation, added barriers, added detection, changed suppression strategy, or different operating procedures.

For project teams, the practical move is to treat the insurer as an early stakeholder. Do not wait until the design has already selected the E S S technology, room, pathway, and protection strategy.
