The North American standards stack has several recurring names.

N F P A 855 is the bellwether installation standard for energy storage systems. The twenty twenty-six revision provides guidance for location, sizing, ventilation, suppression, explosion control, egress, storage, commissioning, and decommissioning.

N F P A 70 is the National Electrical Code. N F P A 70E addresses electrical safety in the workplace. The I C C International Fire Code and N F P A 1 National Fire Code also appear in the broader fire safety landscape.

U L 9540 is the safety standard for energy storage systems and equipment. U L 9540A is the test method for evaluating thermal runaway fire propagation. The paper notes that U L 9540A has been revised to include a large-scale fire test procedure for systems that may be capable of thermal runaway.

C S A C800:25 provides an alternative large-scale fire test methodology. The proposed N F P A 800 battery safety code is expected to address storage, transportation, recycling, and disposal of batteries used in E S S and other equipment.

Together, these references shape product evidence, installation requirements, and review expectations.
