Internationally, the I E C 62933 series is the primary standard family for stationary energy storage systems.

The white paper describes I E C standards as focusing on system-level classification, performance, functional safety, electrical safety, environmental impact, and grid integration, rather than only prescriptive installation requirements.

I E C 62933-1 defines general concepts, classifications, and terminology. I E C 62933-2-1 covers performance testing. The technical specification I E C 62933-2-2 addresses functional safety assessment. I E C 62933-5-1 covers general safety considerations. I E C 62933-5-2 focuses on safety for large-scale electrical energy storage. I E C 62933-5-3 covers grid integration safety.

Other related standards appear as well. I E C 62619 addresses safety requirements for secondary lithium-ion batteries for industrial and U P S applications. I E C 61850 covers communication networks and systems for power utility automation. I E C 61000 addresses electromagnetic compatibility.

Across EMEA and other regions, many national standards are based on or transposed from I E C standards, but permitting and enforcement practices still vary by jurisdiction.
