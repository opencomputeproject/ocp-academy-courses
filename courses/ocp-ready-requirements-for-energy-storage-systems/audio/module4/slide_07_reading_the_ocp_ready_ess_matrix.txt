Appendix A of the white paper gives an O C P Ready requirements matrix for E S S.

For this course, the most useful way to read the matrix is as a planning checklist. It is not just a table to fill out after the design is complete.

The matrix includes fire suppression, segregated white space and grey space, gas and smoke detection, ventilation, and energy storage technology selection.

For fire suppression, the acceptable parameter points to compliance with several N F P A codes and standards or applicable equivalents. The optimum parameter reminds teams that code requirements are the minimum acceptable level of compliance.

For segregated white space and grey space, the matrix points toward water-based sprinkler protection for electrochemical energy storage and consideration of flammable materials.

For detection and ventilation, it points to applicable fire codes, redundant monitoring, alarms, smoke accumulation, automated systems, and enhanced ventilation when needed.

For technology selection, it points teams back to capacity, location, spacing, and fire containment requirements in N F P A 855, with lower fire risk and insurer acceptance as optimum considerations.
