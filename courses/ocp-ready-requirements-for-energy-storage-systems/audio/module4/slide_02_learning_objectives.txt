By the end of this module, you will be able to do four things.

First, you will be able to explain why E S S compliance is fragmented. A system can align with an international standard and still face local code, authority, or insurance constraints.

Second, you will be able to name the major North American standards and codes that appear in the paper, including N F P A 855, N F P A 70, N F P A 70E, I C C and I F C codes, U L 9540, U L 9540A, C S A C800, and the proposed N F P A 800 battery safety code.

Third, you will be able to describe how I E C 62933 and related international standards fit into the E S S landscape.

Fourth, you will be able to use the O C P Ready E S S matrix as a checklist for questions that need design evidence, not as a last-minute form to fill out.

That shift from paperwork to planning is the heart of this module.
