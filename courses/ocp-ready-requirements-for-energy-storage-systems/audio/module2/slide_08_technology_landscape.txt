The white paper compares storage technologies so teams can discuss tradeoffs without turning the paper into a product ranking.

For data centers, physical size for a given energy and power density is a major concern. That is why the paper focuses mainly on electrochemical E S S technologies, while noting that mechanical or gravity systems are less common in typical data center operations.

Within the landscape, the paper includes capacitors, supercapacitors, lithium-ion variants, sodium-ion variants, nickel-zinc batteries, lead-acid batteries, and other battery types. Each one has a different role. Some provide point-of-load decoupling or DC-link ripple handling. Some provide bridge power and pulse buffering. Some provide seconds to minutes of ride-through. Some support minutes to hours of backup energy.

The comparison also includes energy density, power density, cycle life, lifespan, thermal or safety risk, maintenance, response time, and standards relevance.

The important discipline is neutrality. A technology can be appropriate in one location and unacceptable in another. The selection has to be evaluated against the use case, the location, the hazard profile, and the local approval environment.
