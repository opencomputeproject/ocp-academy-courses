By the end of this module, you will be able to do three things.

First, you will be able to distinguish the major deployment models in the white paper: row or rack level E S S, facility-level energy storage, and microgrid or edge models.

Second, you will be able to classify an architecture using the language in the requirements paper. That means identifying whether an E S S is AC-coupled or DC-coupled, centralized or distributed, autonomous or coordinated, energy-oriented or power-oriented, backup-only or grid-interactive.

Third, you will be able to discuss technology families without turning the conversation into a product recommendation. The white paper compares batteries, supercapacitors, capacitors, and other storage mechanisms because each one carries a different combination of runtime, power density, energy density, cycle life, maintenance, and safety considerations.

Those distinctions matter because standards and hazard analysis do not begin with a generic battery. They begin with a specific system, in a specific place, doing a specific job.
