Facility-level E S S sits higher in the power architecture.

The white paper describes centralized U P S systems, containerized battery energy storage systems, and other large-scale E S S units as facility-level energy storage. These systems provide primary backup power when grid supply is lost. When they are interconnected with utility systems, they may also support peak shaving, demand response, or renewable-energy smoothing.

The paper gives a useful threshold for large-scale context: facility-level units greater than 100 kW typically locate in mechanical rooms or in containerized systems and interface with main low-voltage or medium-voltage switchgear.

That placement changes the planning problem. Outdoor containers may need land area, foundations, access roads, clearances, blast protection, and first responder access. Indoor systems may need dedicated rooms, separation, ventilation, detection, fire protection, and structural review.

Facility-level storage can simplify some fault-domain questions because it is more centralized. But it can also increase consequence if one system serves a large load domain. That is why location, sizing, separation, and protection are not afterthoughts.
