Architecture language gives teams a structured way to discuss E S S risk.

Start with electrical coupling. A DC-coupled E S S connects directly to DC distribution paths supplying I T or sub-distribution systems. An AC-coupled E S S interfaces through power conversion systems at the AC level.

Then look at system distribution. Distributed E S S segments energy storage into multiple smaller systems across defined fault domains. Centralized E S S consolidates storage into a single system or a limited number of systems serving larger load domains.

Control architecture is another distinction. Autonomous control means local E S S control with independent protection and limited system-level coordination. Coordinated or hierarchical control connects the E S S to site-level energy management or power control systems.

The paper also distinguishes energy-oriented systems from power-oriented systems, hybrid E S S architectures from single-technology systems, and backup-only systems from grid-interactive systems.

These labels are not academic. They shape protection coordination, safety analysis, standards applicability, operating procedures, and who has to be involved in the approval path.
