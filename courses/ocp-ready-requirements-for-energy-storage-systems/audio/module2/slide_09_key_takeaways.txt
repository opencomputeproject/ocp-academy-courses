Module 2 gives us the vocabulary for describing an E S S deployment.

Row, rack, and sidecar systems move storage closer to the load. Facility-level systems concentrate energy storage higher in the power architecture. Microgrid and edge systems add coordination with local generation and grid interaction.

Architecture labels matter. AC-coupled, DC-coupled, centralized, distributed, autonomous, coordinated, energy-oriented, power-oriented, hybrid, backup-only, and grid-interactive are not just engineering adjectives. They affect safety review, standards applicability, controls, and operations.

Charging energy also matters. Recharge is a site load, and unmanaged charging can create capacity, cost, and reliability impacts.

Finally, technology comparisons should stay practical and neutral. The question is not which chemistry is universally best. The question is which technology fits this use case, this architecture, this location, this risk profile, and this approval path.
