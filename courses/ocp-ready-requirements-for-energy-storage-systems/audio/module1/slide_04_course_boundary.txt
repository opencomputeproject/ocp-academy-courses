This course has a clear boundary.

The white paper is not a buyer's guide for battery chemistry. It does not tell you which battery to purchase, which vendor to select, or which architecture your site must use.

The speakers at the EMEA Summit were explicit about that point. Their goal is to provide neutral guidance about deployment models, common technologies, hazards, standards, and risk considerations. The decision remains application specific.

The white paper also focuses on data center applications, especially behind-the-meter deployments. It does not try to solve the full grid-tied utility interconnection problem. Utility, distribution system operator, and transmission system operator rules can add an entirely different compliance layer.

So the practical question is not, "Which E S S is best?" The better question is, "Given this use case, location, architecture, technology, and region, what hazards and approvals must be planned before the project becomes expensive to change?"

That boundary keeps the course useful. We are learning how to reason through requirements, not how to make a product recommendation.
