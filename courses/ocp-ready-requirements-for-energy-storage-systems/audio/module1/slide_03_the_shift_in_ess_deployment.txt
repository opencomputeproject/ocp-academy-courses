Energy storage is no longer only a large centralized U P S question.

The Summit presentation describes a shift from singular, centralized energy storage toward more distributed deployment across the data center power architecture. That includes facility-level systems, row-level or sidecar systems, rack-level backup battery units, and short-duration buffering close to rapidly changing loads.

This matters because every move closer to the I T load changes the design conversation. The E S S may now sit near equipment rooms, power rooms, white space, grey space, or outdoor yards. It may support multiple fault domains. It may serve sustained ride-through, short-duration power smoothing, or both.

The white paper does not treat that shift as just an electrical architecture topic. It treats it as a safety, operations, permitting, and lifecycle topic.

That is the first mindset for the course. When E S S becomes distributed, responsibility also becomes distributed. The electrical team, facilities team, fire protection team, operations team, authority having jurisdiction, and insurer all need enough shared vocabulary to evaluate the same system.
