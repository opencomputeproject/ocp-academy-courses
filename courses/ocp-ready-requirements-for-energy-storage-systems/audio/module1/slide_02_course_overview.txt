The course has four modules.

First, we frame why E S S requirements matter now, especially as data center power architectures become more distributed and AI loads become more dynamic.

Second, we look at deployment models, architectures, and storage technologies. That module helps you describe where an E S S sits, how it couples electrically, and how the technology choice shapes the compliance path.

Third, we focus on safety and operations. We cover electrical shock risk, thermal runaway, off-gassing, detection, ventilation, suppression, testing, emergency planning, and site design considerations.

Fourth, we turn to codes, standards, and O C P Ready application. That is where we connect N F P A, I E C, U L, C S A, FM Global, local authorities, and the O C P Ready E S S matrix.

The links on this course overview are important. The white paper is the canonical technical source. The O C P Energy project is where the community work continues. The Summit video is a useful companion because the speakers explain why the white paper was written and how the new subgroup expects contributors to use it.
