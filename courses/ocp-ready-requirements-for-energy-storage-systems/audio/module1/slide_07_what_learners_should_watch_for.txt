Throughout the course, use five recurring questions.

First, where is the E S S? Facility level, row level, rack level, sidecar, indoor room, outdoor container, white space, grey space, and yard deployments all create different exposure paths.

Second, what technology is used? Electrochemical batteries, supercapacitors, capacitors, and hybrid systems do not share the same runtime, energy density, maintenance profile, or hazard profile.

Third, what hazards exist? Look for electrical shock, arc flash, thermal runaway, gas release, explosion risk, electrolyte leakage, toxic byproducts, and fire propagation.

Fourth, which codes and standards apply? The answer may include N F P A 855, N F P A 70, N F P A 70E, ANSI CAN U L 9540, U L 9540A, C S A C800, I E C 62933, local fire code, and insurance guidance.

Fifth, who must approve it? A design is not complete until the authority having jurisdiction, relevant certifiers, insurers, and facility operations stakeholders can accept the risk controls.
