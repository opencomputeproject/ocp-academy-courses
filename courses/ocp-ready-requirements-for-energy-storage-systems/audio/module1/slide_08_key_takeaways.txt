The key takeaway from this first module is that E S S is now part of the core data center power architecture.

It supports resilience, ride-through, power smoothing, and new deployment patterns for high-density compute. But it also moves chemical and electrical risk into places where data center teams must plan carefully.

The O C P white paper is useful because it organizes that problem. It does not prescribe a single architecture or chemistry. Instead, it gives practitioners a common way to discuss deployment models, technologies, hazards, codes, standards, and operating considerations.

For the rest of the course, keep the same discipline. Start with the use case and location. Then identify the architecture and storage technology. Then map the hazards. Then bring in the codes, standards, authority having jurisdiction, insurer, and operating plan.

That sequence prevents a common failure mode: discovering after design work is already advanced that the safety or approval path does not match the chosen deployment.
