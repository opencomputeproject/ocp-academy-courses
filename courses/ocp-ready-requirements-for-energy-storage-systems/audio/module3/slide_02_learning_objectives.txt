By the end of this module, you will be able to do four things.

First, you will be able to describe the electrical hazard baseline in the white paper, including the N F P A 70E statement that shock hazard is present at voltages greater than 50 volts.

Second, you will be able to recognize the major E S S hazard categories: thermal runaway, off-gassing, hydrogen and oxygen release, toxic gases, electrolyte leakage, combustible concentration risk, and fire propagation.

Third, you will be able to connect hazards to mitigation categories. That includes location, separation, thermal barriers, smoke detection, gas detection, ventilation, suppression, explosion prevention, and emergency response planning.

Fourth, you will be able to explain why testing, hazard mitigation analysis, trained personnel, personal protective equipment, and documented procedures are part of the system, not paperwork after the design is finished.

This module does not train anyone to service E S S equipment. It gives project stakeholders the shared safety vocabulary they need before trained specialists do that work.
