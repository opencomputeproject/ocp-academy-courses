The main lesson from Module 3 is that E S S safety is a system.

Electrical risk begins well before the voltages now appearing in modern data center E S S deployments. Thermal, gas, explosion, and chemical hazards add more failure modes, especially for electrochemical storage.

Location, separation, fire rating, thermal barriers, detection, ventilation, suppression, and explosion control are interdependent. A control that helps one hazard can affect another, so the design has to be evaluated as a whole.

Testing and hazard mitigation analysis help project teams move from belief to evidence. The authority having jurisdiction and insurers will often care about that evidence, not only the design intent.

Operations complete the picture. Trained personnel, personal protective equipment, de-energizing and tagging, maintenance logs, emergency response plans, and site access are not secondary details. They are how the design remains safe over the facility lifecycle.
