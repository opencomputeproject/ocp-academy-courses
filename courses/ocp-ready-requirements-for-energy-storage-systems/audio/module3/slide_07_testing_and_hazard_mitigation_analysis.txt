Testing and hazard mitigation analysis turn assumptions into evidence.

This slide has several standards names, so listen for the role each one plays. C S A C eight hundred, twenty twenty-five edition, and U L ninety-five forty A, fifth edition, provide large-scale fire test evidence paths. N F P A eight fifty-five is the broader installation and utilization safety standard.

U L ninety-five forty addresses safety for electrochemical energy storage technologies. U L ninety-five forty A is the test method for evaluating thermal runaway fire propagation in battery energy storage systems. The current edition also includes a procedure for a large-scale fire test when an ESS may be capable of thermal runaway.

C S A C eight hundred provides another large-scale fire test methodology.

N F P A eight fifty-five connects that evidence to site requirements. It addresses location, sizing, ventilation, fire suppression, explosion control, egress, storage, commissioning, and decommissioning.

Hazard mitigation analysis, or H M A, brings the evidence together. It evaluates credible failure modes, safety systems, survivability, and authority requirements. For a real project, this is where product evidence, site design, and A H J approval meet.
