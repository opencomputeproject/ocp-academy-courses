Module 3: Safety Hazards, Mitigation, and Operations.

This module connects E S S architecture to the safety controls that make deployment acceptable.

We will cover electrical hazards, thermal risks, gas release, explosion concerns, chemical hazards, location and separation planning, detection, ventilation, suppression, testing, hazard mitigation analysis, emergency response, and civil, structural, and architectural considerations.

The white paper is direct on this point: no battery or other E S S technology is inherently safe. That does not mean E S S should be avoided. It means the deployment has to be engineered, approved, operated, and maintained with the hazard profile in view.

By the end, you should be able to explain why safety is not a single device. It is an integrated design and operations system.
