Location is one of the strongest risk controls.

The white paper includes preferred location guidance and cites FM Global Data Sheet 5-33 for lithium-ion battery energy storage systems. One recommendation is that battery installation should be within a room with a 2-hour fire rating as a minimum.

The paper also discusses separation distances. It references separation between lithium iron phosphate E S S racks and combustible materials, separation from accessible sides of racks, and additional separation from non-accessible sides. Where a first test demonstrates thermal runaway cannot propagate between racks, spacing can be adjusted according to the evidence.

Thermal barriers are another control. Solid fire barriers and solid metal construction can reduce fire risk area and help prevent propagation.

The point is not that every site has the same layout. The point is that location is evidence-based. Teams have to evaluate the chemistry, enclosure, fire rating, access, surrounding combustibles, personnel exposure, and adjacent equipment before selecting a location that an authority or insurer will accept.
