Operations and site design determine whether safety controls remain real after installation.

The white paper states that repairs to E S S must only be made by trained and qualified personnel with appropriate personal protective equipment, and must be documented in maintenance logs. If E S S is decommissioned, the authority having jurisdiction is notified and the plan is executed by trained personnel.

For emergency response, data center personnel should follow facility and manufacturer standard operating procedures. They should not attempt heroic intervention. The paper is direct that E S S fires present immediate life-safety risk, and no fire extinguisher exists that is effective on lithium-ion battery fires.

Civil, structural, and architectural planning also matter. Outdoor containerized B E S S may require substantial land area, foundations, evaluated soils, access roads, clearances, and possibly blast protection. Indoor rack-based lithium-ion B E S S may weigh 4,000 to 6,000 pounds fully loaded.

White space and grey space separation, cable and pipe penetration sealing, gas, water, dust, smoke, and liquid containment all become part of the safety picture.
