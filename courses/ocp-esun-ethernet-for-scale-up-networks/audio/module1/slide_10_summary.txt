Three things to take away. First, "ee-sun" is an evolutionary change to Ethernet, not a new fabric — it leverages the optics, the silicon, and the operational tooling the industry already runs. Second, the central technical move is replacing the I P header with a four-byte "ee-sun" Header that re-introduces just the fields scale-up actually needs: congestion notification, class of service, flow-label entropy, and a hop counter. Third, the fabric story — PFC and CBFC for transient congestion, EH-E C N for persistent, LLR for link resilience, EH-Co S for differentiation, EH-Flow Label for load balancing, and EH-T T L for loop avoidance. It was all wired together by a working group of one hundred sixty-six members spanning AMD, Arista, Arm, Broadcom, Cisco, HPE, Marvell, Meta, Microsoft, Nvidia, Open A I, and Oracle. Version one point zero is the floor, not the ceiling. And "ee-sun" version one point one is already in development, and the working group has committed to backward compatibility as the specification evolves.
