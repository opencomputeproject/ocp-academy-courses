By the end of this course, you'll be able to do five things. First, position scale-up networking against scale-out and front-end networking, and say why each one exists. Second, name the trends that pushed the industry toward a dedicated scale-up specification. Third, walk the four-byte ee-sun Header field by field, and explain what it replaces from the I P header it displaces. Fourth, describe the fabric story end to end — flow control, congestion management, link-level retry, load balancing, and loop avoidance — and how ee-sun puts each piece together. And fifth, list the conformance requirements for endpoints and for switches, and know where security fits in the stack. We'll lean on the spec for the facts and on the OCP Summit talk for the framing. Ready?
