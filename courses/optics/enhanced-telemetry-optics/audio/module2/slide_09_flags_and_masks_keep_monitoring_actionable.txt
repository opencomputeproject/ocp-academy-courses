Flags and masks are how the model stays actionable. Summary flags help software and operators see that a metric group has crossed a threshold or moved into a condition worth attention.

Masks provide tuning. A deployment may decide that a particular counter should not influence the link-health calculation in a certain operating mode, qualification phase, or integration context. The mask lets the deployment include or exclude fields while keeping the broader structure intact.

The balance is important. Too many raw counters create noise. Too much summarization hides cause. Flags and masks sit in the middle: a concise health view with enough control to keep the monitoring signal meaningful.
Masks should be used carefully. Excluding a counter can reduce false alarms, but it can also remove a useful signal. The deployment should document why a field is masked and when that choice should be revisited.
