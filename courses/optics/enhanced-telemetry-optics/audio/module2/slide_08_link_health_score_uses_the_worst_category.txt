Averaging can be comforting and dangerous. If five categories look excellent and one category is failing, an average may still look acceptable. That is not useful for operations, because the failing category can still take the link down.

The spec handles this by selecting the minimum score category for the link-health rating. Put simply, the weakest category drives the reported health. That does not mean every metric has equal meaning in every deployment. It means the summary should not hide the one category that deserves attention.

This is a good reliability principle. A health score should compress information only when it preserves the action signal. If compression masks the failing field, the score becomes a dashboard ornament rather than a troubleshooting tool.
This is why a health score should be explainable. The operator should be able to drill from the score to the category and from the category to the evidence. A score without that path is not enough.
