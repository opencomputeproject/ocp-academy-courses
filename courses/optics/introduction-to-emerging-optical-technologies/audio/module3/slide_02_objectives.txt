By the end of this module, you will be able to evaluate density choices more carefully.

You will explain why fiber count becomes a physical constraint in trays, conduits, racks, and cable pathways. You will describe the basic multicore fiber idea, where multiple independent cores share one fiber structure.

You will also explain why fan-in and fan-out devices matter. They are bridge components that let existing single-core interfaces connect to denser multicore trunks while the ecosystem matures.

Finally, you will compare testing implications for multicore and hollow-core fiber. The goal is not to memorize every test. The goal is to remember that new fiber structures create new inspection and characterization assumptions.
