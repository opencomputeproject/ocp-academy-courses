Module three is about density and testability in the optical plant.

AI infrastructure can require much more fiber than conventional designs. That creates pressure in overhead pathways, rack cable managers, conduit, labels, bend radius, and installation labor.

Multicore fiber is one response to that pressure. It can increase the number of optical paths inside a smaller physical outline. But density alone is not enough. The link still has to be connected, mapped, inspected, certified, and troubleshot.

This module focuses on that balance: more compact fiber infrastructure that remains operationally understandable.
