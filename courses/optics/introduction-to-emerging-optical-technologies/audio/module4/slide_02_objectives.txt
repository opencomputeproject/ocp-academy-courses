By the end of this module, you will be able to compare emerging maturity with more discipline.

You will explain why hollow-core fiber can reduce propagation latency compared with conventional solid-core fiber. You will describe the plain-language idea behind anti-resonant guidance without needing the detailed equations.

You will identify where lower latency matters: especially in scale-across designs and tightly synchronized distributed workloads.

You will also explain why a QPU is best understood as a specialized accelerator in a hybrid compute stack, not a replacement for every CPU or GPU.

Finally, you will compare CPO, expanded beam, MCF, HCF, and QPU integration by adoption horizon and ecosystem readiness.
