Module four looks at latency, quantum, and the adoption horizon.

The first three modules focused on scale, efficiency, serviceability, and density. Now we look at technologies that sit closer to the edge of deployment planning.

Hollow-core fiber targets propagation latency by changing the medium that carries the light. QPU integration targets specialized compute by connecting quantum processors into a classical accelerated system.

Neither topic should be treated as magic. The useful question is where the technology fits, what problem it relieves, and what ecosystem maturity is still required.
