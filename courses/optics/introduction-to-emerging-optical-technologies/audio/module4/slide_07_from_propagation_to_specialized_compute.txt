Until now, speed has meant propagation: how quickly an optical signal crosses distance. The next section uses speed differently. It asks where specialized compute may accelerate work that classical processors handle poorly.

Hollow-core fiber and quantum processing units are not one deployment path, and one does not depend on the other. Their connection in this module is the systems lesson.

HCF's lower-latency promise matters only when cable, connection, testing, and manufacturing ecosystems are ready. A QPU becomes useful only when classical processors, control software, and low-latency interconnect can operate it reliably.

This transition brings us back to the module goals. Treat the QPU as a specialized accelerator rather than a replacement for CPUs or GPUs. Then use the same adoption lens we used for HCF: what problem does it solve, and what surrounding ecosystem must mature?
