You have worked through the pressures behind AI optical infrastructure: scale, efficiency, density, latency, serviceability, and specialized compute integration.

You have also built a practical map. CPO changes the electrical-to-optical boundary. Expanded beam and blind mating change service workflows. Multicore fiber changes physical density. That map will help you evaluate future optical claims with the right question: what constraint does this technology relieve, and what must mature before it can scale?

As you continue through the series, keep this course as the map. When a later course discusses cable construction, installation, splicing, panels, or connector hygiene, those details connect back to the same pressures introduced here.

The future of optical infrastructure will not be one breakthrough replacing everything else. It will be many practical improvements layered together: better packaging, denser fiber, cleaner mating, lower latency, better testing, and better operational visibility. The teams that understand those layers will be better prepared to design and run AI infrastructure at scale.
