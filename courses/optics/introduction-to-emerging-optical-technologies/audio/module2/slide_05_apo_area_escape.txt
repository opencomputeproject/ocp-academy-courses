A CPO roadmap is partly a story about placement: front panel, near package, on package, and deeper into the package. Areal Packaged Optics, or APO, pushes that idea one step further by changing the escape geometry.

In many optical connectivity approaches, I/O leaves around the edge of the die or package. That perimeter can become a constraint. As bandwidth grows, designers are trying to fit more lanes along a limited shoreline.

APO changes the mental model. If the optical engine sits under the die and optical I/O can leave through the die face, then scaling is tied more to area than to perimeter. That can free package edge space for HBM, power delivery, and other dense electrical interfaces.

The trade is that APO is much more aggressive. It implies 3D integration, very fine pitch, difficult thermal and manufacturing questions, and a different field-repair story.

For this course, treat APO as a future-facing endpoint on the integration continuum. It helps explain why packaging geometry is becoming part of optical architecture.
