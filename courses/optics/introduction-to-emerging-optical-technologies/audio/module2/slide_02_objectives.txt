By the end of this module, you will be able to classify four related design pressures.

You will explain why long high-speed electrical paths are expensive in power and thermal headroom. You will describe the difference between a pluggable optic, a near-packaged optic, and a co-packaged optic at a conceptual level.

You will also explain why expanded beam connectivity changes the contamination problem at the connector interface.

Finally, you will connect rear blind mating to operations. The point is not simply where the connector mates. The point is faster replacement, less front-panel fiber handling, and shorter recovery.
