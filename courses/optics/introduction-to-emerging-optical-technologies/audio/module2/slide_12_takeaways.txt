The main lesson in this module is that efficiency is a system property.

CPO targets electrical reach, power, thermal headroom, and faceplate density. Those benefits are compelling, but they come with harder serviceability, packaging, laser, and interoperability questions.

EBO and PTMT-style lensed ferrules target a different pain point: contamination sensitivity, repeated mating, and the time it takes to turn up or restore dense optical environments. Rear blind mating turns that connector behavior into a maintenance strategy.

The practical question is not whether one technology is good or bad. It is whether the technology relieves the constraint that matters most in the system you are designing, and whether the operations model is ready for it.

The deeper pattern is that integration and serviceability pull in opposite directions. Greater integration can improve power and density, but it can make individual parts harder to access. Better service workflows can reduce downtime, but they require mechanical and optical interfaces designed for repeated use.

A mature architecture has to balance both. It is not enough to ask whether the lab prototype works. The better question is whether the system can be installed, monitored, repaired, and scaled by real teams under real operational pressure.
