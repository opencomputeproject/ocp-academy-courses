This course follows four questions.

First, why are AI systems changing the optical infrastructure problem? That gives us the context: scale-up, scale-out, scale-across, and the five drivers behind emerging optical architecture.

Second, how are power, thermal design, and serviceability changing where optics live? That is where co-packaged optics, near-packaged optics, expanded beam connectivity, and rear blind mating come in.

Third, how does the cable plant handle much higher fiber density while staying testable? That is the role of multicore fiber, fan-in and fan-out, and new inspection assumptions.

Fourth, what belongs in the longer horizon? We will cover hollow-core fiber for latency and QPU integration for specialized compute, then compare the adoption maturity of the technologies as a portfolio.
