The key takeaway from this module is that bandwidth is only one part of the emerging optics story.

AI infrastructure creates pressure across several dimensions at once: scale, efficiency, density, latency, and serviceability. Scale-up, scale-out, and scale-across each emphasize a different part of that problem.

Rack architecture also matters. Power, cooling, mechanical access, and optical routing now interact early in the design process.

That is why we will evaluate emerging technologies by the constraint they relieve. Co-packaged optics, expanded beam, multicore fiber, hollow-core fiber, and QPU integration are all important, but they are not solving the same problem.

Another way to say it is this: the optical layer is becoming a shared design surface. Electrical engineers, mechanical engineers, facilities teams, optics teams, software teams, and operations teams all feel the consequences of the same technology choices.

That is the reason the rest of the course is organized by constraints instead of product categories. We will ask what each technology changes, what it makes easier, what it makes harder, and what has to mature before it can scale across real deployments.
