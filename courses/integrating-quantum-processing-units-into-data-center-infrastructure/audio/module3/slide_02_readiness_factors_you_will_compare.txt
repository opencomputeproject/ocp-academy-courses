# pronunciation: QPU -> "Q P U"; EMI -> "E M I"; EMC -> "E M C"
By the end of this module, you'll be able to compare facility readiness factors across quantum modalities.

You'll begin with site selection. That includes geography, vibration, electromagnetic noise, delivery paths, structure, power, cooling, and environmental stability.

Then you'll interpret service boundaries. A standard data center often draws the tenant boundary around the rack. Quantum systems can blur that line because they may need cryogens, vendor access, special maintenance, environmental measurement, or facility-level safety controls.

You'll also read the modality matrix like an operator. Instead of memorizing every entry, you'll learn what the categories mean: cryogenics, magnetic-field sensitivity, vibration, humidity, temperature, power draw, and auxiliary requirements.

Finally, you'll identify risks that affect both directions: protecting the Q P U system from the facility, and protecting the facility from the Q P U system.
