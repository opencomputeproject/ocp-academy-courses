# pronunciation: QPU -> "Q P U"; QPUs -> "Q P U systems"; EMI -> "E M I"; EMC -> "E M C"
Module three turned modality diversity into facility planning.

You saw that site selection starts with the world around the building: vibration, electromagnetic noise, climate, access, and geography. You also saw that normal data center demarcation can be too vague for quantum, especially when cryogens, vendor service, water, optical stability, or environmental monitoring enter the workflow.

The modality comparison matters because Q P U systems do not share one operating envelope. A photonic appliance, a laser-based neutral atom system, a trapped-ion system, a dilution-fridge-based superconducting system, and a room-temperature diamond system all ask different things from the facility.

The larger principle is two-way protection. The facility must protect the Q P U system from disturbance, and the Q P U system must not introduce unmanaged risk to surrounding infrastructure.
