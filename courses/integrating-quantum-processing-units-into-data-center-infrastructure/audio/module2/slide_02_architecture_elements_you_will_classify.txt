By the end of this module, you'll be able to classify the major elements in the logical Q P U stack.

You'll start with the full-stack view, from the physical Q P U through signal delivery, control, middleware, and application integration. Then you'll separate the four main logical Q P U components: real-time host, interconnect, quantum system controller, and physical Q P U.

You'll also explain why classical infrastructure is not optional. Quantum workloads need orchestration, preprocessing, post-processing, control, observability, error handling, and storage.

Finally, you'll compare the three deployment coupling models: standalone, co-located, and on-node integration. Those models help you reason about latency, complexity, and what a facility must be ready to support.
