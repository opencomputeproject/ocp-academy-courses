# pronunciation: QPU -> "Q P U"; QEC -> "Q E C"
Module two showed why the word hybrid is not decoration.

A Q P U system depends on a stack of classical functions. The real-time host, interconnect, controller, physical Q P U, middleware, A I resources, H P C systems, storage, and networks all shape performance and operability.

You also saw that different classical functions have different urgency. Some tasks, such as A I-assisted preprocessing and post-processing, can be less latency-sensitive. Q E C and control loops can be much tighter.

The storage example is the clearest reminder. Even when application output is small, the system may create large volumes of operational data. Quantum infrastructure planning must include control, latency, observability, and storage from the start.
