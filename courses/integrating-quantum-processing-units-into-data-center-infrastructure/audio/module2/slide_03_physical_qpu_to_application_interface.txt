# pronunciation: QPU -> "Q P U"; QPUs -> "Q P U systems"; API -> "A P I"; APIs -> "A P I interfaces"
A quantum computer is not just the chip or the trap or the optical system where quantum information lives.

The whitepaper describes a full-stack architecture. At the bottom is the physical Q P U and its operating environment. Above that is signal delivery. Depending on the modality, those signals may be microwave, optical, electronic, or some combination.

Control and readout systems translate digital instructions into precise physical actions and measurements. Middleware coordinates hybrid workflows, including classical pre-processing, quantum execution, and post-processing. Application interfaces and A P I interfaces connect the system to H P C, cloud, and A I environments.

That stack is tightly coupled. Noise, timing, calibration, and environmental stability all affect whether the physical system can deliver useful results. For a data center team, the lesson is that a Q P U system must be evaluated as a complete operational stack, not as an isolated accelerator.

This is also where open interfaces become important. If the application layer, resource managers, controllers, and facility systems cannot exchange useful state, then operators cannot treat the Q P U system as managed infrastructure. They can only treat it as a special case. The whitepaper's architecture gives the community a vocabulary for moving away from special cases.
