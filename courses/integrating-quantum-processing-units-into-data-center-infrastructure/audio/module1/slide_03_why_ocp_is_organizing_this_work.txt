# pronunciation: OCP -> "O C P"; FTI -> "F T I"; QPU -> "Q P U"; QPUs -> "Q P U systems"
O C P is organizing this work because quantum is leaving the lab faster than data center practice can standardize around it.

The workstream sits inside the O C P Future Technologies Initiative. Its full name is Data Center Integration of Quantum Information Infrastructure. That title matters. The scope is not just quantum computing as a scientific discipline. It is the integration of quantum information technologies into open, general-purpose data center environments.

The whitepaper is an early community framework. It brings together Q P U vendors, data center stakeholders, systems integrators, cryogenic providers, and O C P contributors. Some of those contributors represent competing technologies. That makes the common-ground work more valuable.

The point is to identify where modalities differ, where they are similar, and what infrastructure teams can reasonably plan for. Without that common language, every deployment becomes a bespoke negotiation between a facility, a vendor, and a workload owner.

That is why the workstream's scope includes physical and logical requirements. Physical requirements cover the facility envelope: power, cooling, access, zoning, vibration, and environmental controls. Logical requirements cover how quantum resources are discovered, scheduled, monitored, connected, and managed alongside classical infrastructure. The whitepaper treats both sides as part of the same systems problem.
