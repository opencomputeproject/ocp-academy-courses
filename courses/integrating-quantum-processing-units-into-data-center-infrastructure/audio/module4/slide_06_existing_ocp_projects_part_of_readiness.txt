# pronunciation: OCP -> "O C P"; QPU -> "Q P U"; QPUs -> "Q P U systems"; EMI -> "E M I"
Existing O C P projects are part of quantum readiness.

The whitepaper identifies several non-quantum O C P areas that form the backbone of deployment. Facility work defines vibration, E M I, spatial zoning, access control, and environmental stability. Hardware Management supports discovery, configuration, telemetry, control, asynchronous events, and fault handling.

Networking affects control-plane and data-plane scalability, latency, synchronization, and bandwidth. Cooling Environments define thermal operating envelopes, cryogenic integration, heat rejection, and thermal stability. Rack and Power shape grounding, electrical stability, power distribution, and density.

Sustainability matters because quantum must scale without wasteful duplication. Storage supports workflow persistence and post-processing. The Time Appliance Project provides timing and synchronization needed for coordinated control and error-correction feedback.

The message is encouraging. Quantum does not need to invent every practice from scratch. It needs to connect specialized requirements to mature infrastructure domains.

This is also where O C P's community model matters. A quantum vendor may understand its device deeply. A facility operator may understand power, cooling, safety, and serviceability deeply. A hardware management group may understand telemetry and fault handling. The integration challenge is to bring those domains into the same specification conversation.
