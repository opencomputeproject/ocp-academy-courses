Pilot deployments are necessary, but they are not enough.

The whitepaper describes a coordination gap. Facility design cycles, infrastructure investment horizons, and quantum hardware timelines are not naturally aligned. It also notes that data-center planning horizons are moving from the traditional 2 to 3 years toward 5 to 10 years.

That timing matters. If a facility is designed without understanding future quantum requirements, it may not be ready when Q P U systems need to scale. If quantum vendors design only for bespoke environments, facilities may struggle to support them safely and economically.

The summit field perspective adds urgency. Operators are already thinking about where quantum fits beside A I and H P C, where there is space, where there is power, where chilled water is available, and where latency-sensitive workloads actually live.

Production readiness starts before the purchase order. It starts in planning assumptions.

This is why the whitepaper emphasizes data-center fungibility. Facilities designed today may need to host new categories of specialized compute years from now. If the industry can define common envelopes early, operators can preserve optionality. If it cannot, quantum deployment may be constrained by buildings that were never prepared for the requirements.
