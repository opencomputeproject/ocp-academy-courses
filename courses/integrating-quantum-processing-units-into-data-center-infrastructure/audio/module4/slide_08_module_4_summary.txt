# pronunciation: OCP -> "O C P"; QPU -> "Q P U"; QPUs -> "Q P U systems"
Module four brought the course back to production practice.

You saw that pilot deployments are valuable, but production quantum requires repeatability. Service boundaries, operating envelopes, calibration, consumables, maintenance, controls, and shared infrastructure all need to become explicit.

You also connected quantum requirements to existing O C P domains. Facilities, hardware management, networking, cooling, rack and power, sustainability, storage, and timing all influence Q P U reliability and scalability.

The core lesson is systems engineering. Q P U systems are not only scientific instruments and not only accelerators. They are specialized infrastructure assets that must be integrated with the building, the classical compute stack, and the operational model.
