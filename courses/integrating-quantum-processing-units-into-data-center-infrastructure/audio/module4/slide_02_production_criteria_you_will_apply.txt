# pronunciation: OCP -> "O C P"; QPU -> "Q P U"; QPUs -> "Q P U systems"
By the end of this module, you'll be able to apply production-readiness criteria to a Q P U deployment.

You'll explain why pilot installations are useful but not enough. Early deployments teach the industry what can work. Production deployment requires repeatable boundaries, predictable maintenance, standard interfaces, and clear operational expectations.

You'll connect quantum needs to existing O C P project areas: Facility, Hardware Management, Networking, Cooling Environments, Rack and Power, Sustainability, Storage, and the Time Appliance Project.

You'll also describe shared infrastructure as a scaling idea. Centralized cryogenic plant resources are one example, but the broader principle is coordinated design.

Finally, you'll leave with a practical checklist that helps turn the whitepaper into a planning conversation.
