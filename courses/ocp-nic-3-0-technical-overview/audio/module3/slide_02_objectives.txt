# pronunciation: 4C+ -> four C plus; DSFF -> D S F F; PCIe -> P C I E; FRU EEPROM -> Fru E E Prom

By the end of this module, you will be able to map the four C plus connector as a set of signal groups, not just a pin table. You will compare S F F and D S F F connector usage, lane ranges, and management-path assumptions.

You will also be able to separate baseboard-owned signals from card-owned signals. That distinction is the key to understanding present detection, bifurcation, power enable behavior, and Nick power good.

Finally, you will connect P C I E bifurcation to Fru E E Prom discovery, walk through the mandatory power states, and keep hot-swap implementation scope on the correct side of the platform boundary.
