# pronunciation: FRU EEPROM -> Fru E E Prom; ASIC -> ay-sick; PRSNTB -> presence B

Two power rails come through the edge connector. The 3.3 volt edge rail has plus or minus nine percent tolerance and supports auxiliary circuitry, the management controller, and the Fru E E Prom. The 12 volt edge rail has an asymmetric tolerance of plus eight and minus twelve percent, and powers the main ay-sick, PHY, and high-speed circuitry.

Slot power envelopes run from 15 watts up to 160 watts, and each card declares its maximum draw in the Fru E E Prom so the baseboard can enforce limits.

For hot swap, keep the scope precise. The card exposes the required control and presence signals. The dedicated hot-swap controller behavior is primarily a platform-side design responsibility. That boundary matters because it keeps card compliance from being confused with a full baseboard power-controller implementation.
