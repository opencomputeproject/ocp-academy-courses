# pronunciation: PRSNTB -> presence B; FRU EEPROM -> Fru E E Prom; PERST -> P E reset; NIC_PWR_GOOD -> Nick power good; WAKE# -> Wake

The OCP Nick three point zero power model has four mandatory normal states: Nick Power Off, ID Mode, Aux Power Mode, and Main Power Mode. Programming Mode is optional and exists for field update behavior. It should not be treated as the next normal operating step after Main Power.

In ID Mode, the card is present, presence B is asserted, and the baseboard can read the Fru E E Prom. The 3.3 volt edge rail is active in ID, Aux, and Main Power modes. The 12 volt edge rail is on in Main Power, and may be optionally available in ID or Programming Mode depending on platform behavior.

During Aux Power, P E reset is asserted low, and Nick power good first asserts when the auxiliary-domain rails are up and within tolerance. Version one dot six point zero also clarifies the optional Wake and N C S I over R B T behavior in this part of the sequence.
