# pronunciation: SFF -> S F F; DSFF -> D S F F; PCIe -> P C I E; REFCLK -> ref clock; PERST -> P E reset; FRU EEPROM -> Fru E E Prom

The S F F electrical model is one Primary four C plus connector. That connector carries lanes zero through fifteen, ref clock and P E reset signals zero through three, the main management path, and the Fru E E Prom path used for card identity and capability information.

D S F F extends the lane map by adding a Secondary connector position. The Primary connector covers lanes zero through fifteen. The Secondary connector can cover lanes sixteen through thirty-one, along with the additional ref clock and P E reset mappings needed for those links.

The important mental model is that D S F F is still one card-level design. A single power domain spans both connectors, and the Primary connector remains the anchor for identity and management. Do not treat the Secondary connector as a fully redundant independent management channel.
