# pronunciation: 4C+ -> four C plus; PERST -> P E reset; PRSNTB -> presence B; REFCLK -> ref clock; NC-SI -> N C S I; RBT -> R B T; SMBus -> S M Bus; MCTP -> M C T P

The four C plus connector becomes easier to reason about when you group the signals by purpose. The high-speed group carries P C I E lanes, ref clock, and P E reset. The detection and topology group uses presence B and BIF pins so the baseboard and card agree on what is installed and how the lanes should divide.

Power and sequencing signals form the next group: 3.3 volt edge, 12 volt edge, aux power enable, main power enable, and Nick power good. These signals determine when the card can identify itself, latch topology, and move into normal operation.

The OCP Bay extension adds sideband and service behavior. That includes S M Bus, M C T P bindings, USB, Wake, N C S I over R B T, slot identification, and scan-chain access for platform test, debug, and service workflows.
