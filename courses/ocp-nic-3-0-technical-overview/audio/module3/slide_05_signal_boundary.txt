# pronunciation: PRSNTB -> presence B; PRSNTA -> presence A; PERST -> P E reset; REFCLK -> ref clock; BIF -> B I F; AUX_PWR_EN -> aux power enable; MAIN_PWR_EN -> main power enable; NIC_PWR_GOOD -> Nick power good; SMBus -> S M Bus; MCTP -> M C T P; NC-SI -> N C S I; RBT -> R B T; FRU EEPROM -> Fru E E Prom

This is the integration checklist slide. The baseboard supplies the rails, drives ref clock and P E reset, drives the BIF pins with aux power enable, and controls aux and main power enable. It also reads presence B encoding and reads the Fru E E Prom while the card is in ID Mode.

The card has its own responsibilities. It straps presence B to presence A according to the encoding rules, exposes Fru E E Prom records, latches BIF values under the specified power conditions, and asserts Nick power good when the relevant rails are within tolerance.

The shared sideband path depends on what the card and platform implement. S M Bus, M C T P, N C S I over R B T, R B T isolate, USB, and Wake are not just labels in a pin table. They are boundaries between platform design, card capability, and management policy.
