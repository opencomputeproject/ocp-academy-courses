# review_status: upgraded for Leo v2 narration

OCP Nick 3.0 cards must operate in two airflow orientations. In a hot aisle configuration, air enters from the card edge at typically 55 degrees Celsius because the air has already passed through other server components. Airflow ranges from 50 to 300 linear feet per minute. This is the more thermally challenging orientation. In a cold aisle configuration, air enters from the faceplate side with fresh air at 25 to 35 degrees Celsius. Airflow ranges from 50 to 200 linear feet per minute, and cold aisle provides roughly 60% more power headroom. A single Nick design should work in both orientations. There is no airflow on the bottom side of the card. All cooling must come from the top side heat sink.
