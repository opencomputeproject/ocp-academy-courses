# pronunciation: FRU EEPROM -> Fru E E Prom; 0xC0 -> C zero; 0xD0 -> D zero; 0xD1 -> D one; 0xD2 -> D two; 0xD3 -> D three

Every OCP Nick three point zero card includes exactly one Fru E E Prom on the Primary connector S M Bus interface. It uses the I P M I Fru information storage format and is write-protected in production.

Record C zero is the Base OEM record. It declares form factor, management type, P C I E lane count, power requirements, and cooling tier. Records D zero and D one define D S F F P C I E link subdivision. Record D two is the Extended OEM record for additional capability flags, and record D three describes Flex I O mappings. The baseboard reads this data during ID Mode, before enabling the full main-power path.
