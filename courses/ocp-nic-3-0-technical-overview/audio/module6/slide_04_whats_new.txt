Version one dot six point zero is most useful as an implementation checklist. First, it keeps P C I E Gen six and 64 G T per second signal-integrity work tied to OCP-specific fixture and insertion-loss context. Do not substitute a generic C E M budget for the OCP channel and fixture model.

Second, D S F F capability discovery is more explicit. Presence B encoding identifies the card pattern, then Fru E E Prom OEM records D zero, D one, and D two describe supported link subdivision behavior.

Third, the power-state history clarifies Wake and N C S I over R B T behavior in Aux Mode and removes Wake from Main Power Mode assumptions. The Fru record map also moves Extended OEM and Flex I O records to D two and D three to avoid M H S M P N P conflicts.

Finally, L F F is deprecated. It stays in Appendix nine for historical reference, while active implementation should focus on S F F and D S F F paths.
