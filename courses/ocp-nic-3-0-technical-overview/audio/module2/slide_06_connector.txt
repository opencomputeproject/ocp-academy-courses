# pronunciation: 4C+ -> four C plus; 2C+ -> two C plus; SFF-TA-1002 -> S F F T A one thousand two

The four C plus connector is the heart of the OCP Nick three point zero interface. It follows S F F T A one thousand two and provides the shared edge connector used across compliant cards and slots. A key feature is two-stage mating: power pins make contact first, then signal pins engage.

A full four C plus implementation supports up to 16 P C I E lanes. For lower lane-count cards, a two C plus downshift may be used. That reduced pinout still carries most management signals; it mainly removes lanes eight through fifteen plus specific signals such as USB, power brake, and PRSNTB three. For D S F F, both connector locations remain mechanically important even when lane count is lower.
