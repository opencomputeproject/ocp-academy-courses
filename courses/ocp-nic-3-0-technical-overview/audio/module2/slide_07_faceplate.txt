# review_status: upgraded for Leo v2 narration

The specification defines three retention mechanisms for securing cards in the baseboard. The pull tab is a low-profile, compact mechanism ideal for high-density deployments where clearance between cards is minimal. The ejector latch is a lever mechanism that provides greater mechanical advantage for card extraction, particularly useful for DSFF cards where dual connectors increase insertion force. The internal lock prevents accidental card removal from vibration or handling during transport and operation. On the baseboard side, two connector mounting options exist. Right angle mount positions the receptacle perpendicular to the baseboard, with the card inserting horizontally from the faceplate side. Straddle mount positions the receptacle parallel to the baseboard edge, with the card sitting on top for vertical insert chassis designs.
