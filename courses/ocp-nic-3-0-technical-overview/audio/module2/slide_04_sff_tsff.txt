# pronunciation: SFF -> S F F; TSFF -> T S F F; 4C+ -> four C plus; PCIe -> P C I E

S F F and T S F F are best understood as the same electrical card family with different vertical clearance. Both use one four C plus Primary Connector. Both support up to 16 P C I E lanes. Both share the same 80 watt card-edge power ceiling.

The difference is mechanical. S F F uses the standard faceplate height. T S F F keeps the same footprint and connector behavior, but raises the faceplate and topside space for taller optical cages, larger heatsinks, or taller board components.

That distinction matters during platform planning. If a baseboard and chassis have enough Z height, the tall variant gives the card designer more room without changing the pinout model. If the chassis does not have that clearance, the same electrical design still needs to fit inside the S F F envelope.
