# review_status: upgraded for Leo v2 narration

OCP Nick 3.0 defines five form factors, though one has been deprecated. SFF, the small form factor, is 76 millimeters wide, supports 16 P C I E lanes, and handles up to 80 watts. TSFF, the tall small form factor, shares those same specs but adds a taller faceplate for larger optical modules. DSFF, the dual small form factor, doubles the width to 157.55 millimeters, doubles the lanes to 32, and doubles the power to 160 watts. TDSFF is the tall variant of DSFF. The fifth form factor, LFF, was removed in version version one point six point zero and is no longer recommended for new designs. All form factors use the S F F T A one thousand two-4 C+ connector and support P C I E Gen 6.
