Diablo 400 keeps management and telemetry flexible, but it still tells implementers what kinds of connections matter.

The sidecar or cabinet control module can serve as a dedicated building block that interfaces with all Diablo 400 building blocks. A standard management interface based on Redfish is recommended, although proprietary interfaces are allowed. External interface options include Ethernet, and internal interface options include CAN bus. The specification also allows implementers to use building-block communication ports as provided by their manufacturers.

For telemetry, the specification allows standard interfaces such as Ethernet, I squared C, PMBus, Modbus, and CAN bus. Communication may need to cover sidecar to data center network, sidecar to sidecar, and sidecar to IT rack paths. Inside the rack, communication between building blocks and the sidecar control module is required.

The environmental requirements are equally important. The Diablo 400 power rack and its internal building blocks are designed to be fully air-cooled. The preferred ambient temperature range is minus 5 degrees Celsius to 45 degrees Celsius. The required range is minus 5 degrees Celsius to 35 degrees Celsius. Relative humidity is 10 percent to 90 percent, non-condensing, and altitude without derating is up to 3050 meters, or 10,000 feet.

At the rack level, airflow is front-to-back. Containment features should prevent hot-air recirculation, and optional safety doors must be included in the pressure-drop and fan-sizing work.
