The interface layer starts with the AC PDU and ends at the cable connection to the IT rack.

On the input side, the AC PDU takes AC power from the overhead busway and feeds the power shelves. The specification calls for the AC PDU to accept up to twelve 200 amp cords, include protection fuses, support lug or connectorized input, and optionally include an AC panel or cabinet.

On the output side, the DC PDU output protection and control module attaches to the plus-minus 400 volts DC or 800 volts DC busbar and transfers power to the IT rack through output connectors. It is designed to support up to 400 kilowatts of output, with built-in manageability, output on-off control, protection, telemetry, and no-voltage contact behavior on the output cables.

The output module supports 50 kilowatt and 100 kilowatt cable options. Each output connection includes protection elements such as fuses and relays, physical on-off switches, and LED indicators. Before a cable is unplugged, the output voltage is disconnected.

Power shelves sit behind this interface. They can be up to two OU high, host multiple AC to DC power supply units, support droop and active current sharing, and use thermal sensors on bus clip connectors. The power supply modules are front-accessible, hot-swappable, hot-pluggable, and galvanically isolated from AC input and earth or chassis.

The vertical busbar ties the rack together. It is a single-zone, three-conductor busbar distributing plus 400 volts, minus 400 volts, and common, with support for up to one point one megawatts average load power.
