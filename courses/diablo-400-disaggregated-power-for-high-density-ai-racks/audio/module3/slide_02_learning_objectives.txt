By the end of this module, you'll be able to explain how Diablo 400 handles the practical interface layer.

You'll understand how the AC PDU, DC PDU, AC to DC power shelves, vertical busbar, and output cables work together inside the rack. You'll be able to describe the two grounding choices in the specification: high resistance midpoint grounding and solid contact midpoint grounding.

You'll also be able to identify the recommended management and telemetry interfaces, and the environmental assumptions for an air-cooled sidecar.

Finally, you'll be able to name the major safety and compliance categories that implementers need to account for: short-circuit current rating, cable ratings, accessibility, creepage and clearance, overcurrent protection, protective earthing, leakage current, ground fault detection, energy storage safety, EMC, and environmental regulations.
