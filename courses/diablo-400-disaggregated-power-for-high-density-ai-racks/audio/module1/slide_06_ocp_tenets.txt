The Diablo 400 specification frames the work through the OCP tenets: openness, efficiency, impact, scale, and sustainability.

Openness starts with shared interfaces. The specification is intended to provide a high-level base for a disaggregated power rack delivering plus-minus 400 volts DC to a nearby IT rack. It also names the goal of standardizing high-voltage connectors and mating interfaces.

Efficiency comes from transferring power at higher voltage, reducing I squared R losses, and creating a path toward better end-to-end power distribution. Impact comes from the fact that Diablo 400 is the first disaggregated power rack specification, with the potential to align supply chains and encourage common high-voltage rack architecture.

Scale is about rated power, redundancy schemes, backup power, and the option to parallel power racks. Sustainability is about reuse. If the IT rack changes from one generation to the next, the power rack can potentially remain useful across multiple generations and SKUs.

This is also why the Rack & Power project matters. Diablo 400 belongs inside a community process where operators, suppliers, and standards stakeholders can keep refining the shared infrastructure layer.
