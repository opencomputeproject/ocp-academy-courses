The key idea from this module is that Diablo 400 is a response to density.

AI racks are pushing power from familiar cloud-rack levels into hundreds of kilowatts and toward 1 megawatt class infrastructure. At those power levels, current, copper, heat, and rack space become system-level constraints. Higher-voltage DC distribution helps reduce current for the same power level, but the larger architectural move is disaggregation.

By moving conversion, distribution, protection, and optional energy storage into a sidecar power rack, Diablo 400 lets the IT rack focus on compute, networking, and cooling. And by defining common interfaces, it gives the OCP community a foundation for interoperability and supplier scale.

Next, we will move from motivation into architecture: the physical rack, AC input, DC output, dynamic loading, ride-through, and efficiency requirements that make up the Diablo 400 power path.
