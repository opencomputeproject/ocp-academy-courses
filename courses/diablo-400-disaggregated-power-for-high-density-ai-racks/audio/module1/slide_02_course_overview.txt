This course has a short required core, plus one optional extension.

The core learning path has three modules.

Module 1 explains why Diablo 400 exists. We will cover the rise of AI rack density, the limits of lower-voltage rack distribution, and the OCP context around open standardization.

Module 2 moves into the Diablo 400 architecture itself. That includes the physical rack, AC input, DC output, ride-through, energy storage, dynamic loading, and efficiency requirements.

Module 3 covers the practical implementation topics: PDUs, PSU shelves, cables, grounding, telemetry, environmental operation, safety, EMC, and compliance.

After that, there is an optional future-work module.

That optional module steps beyond the rack-level Diablo 400 sidecar. It looks at broader low voltage direct current, or LVDC, distribution at the data center facility level.

Here is the target for the required core.

After about 35 minutes, you should be able to read the Diablo 400 specification with confidence. You should understand the role of the sidecar power rack. And you should know which tables and design topics deserve careful attention during implementation.
