By the end of this optional module, you'll be able to explain why Diablo 400 is a step toward a larger DC power conversation.

You'll be able to distinguish rack-level sidecar power from facility-level low voltage DC distribution. You'll understand the staged view in the LVDC white paper: start with IT racks, expand to related cooling and infrastructure, and eventually evaluate fuller DC distribution across the data center.

You'll also be able to recognize why voltage classes, voltage bands, polarity, and earthing choices matter. These are not abstract standards topics. They determine which devices can interoperate, how faults behave, and how operators design for safety.

Finally, you'll be able to name the future-work areas that need industry alignment: protection, conversion, energy storage, grid interaction, DC arc flash, and system-level standards.
