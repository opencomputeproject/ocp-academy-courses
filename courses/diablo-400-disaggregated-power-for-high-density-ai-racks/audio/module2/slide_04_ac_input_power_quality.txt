The AC input side of Diablo 400 is designed around three-phase input into an AC power distribution unit, or AC PDU.

That AC PDU distributes power to the AC to DC power supply modules. The rack can support a single three-phase AC input feed, or dual AC input for N plus N redundancy. The specification covers both Wye and Delta input options. For Wye, the nominal line-to-line inputs are 480, 415, and 400 volts R M S, with plus or minus 10 percent tolerance. The corresponding line-to-neutral voltages are 277, 240, and 230 volts R M S, also with plus or minus 10 percent tolerance. For Delta, the nominal three-phase inputs are again 480, 415, and 400 volts R M S, with plus or minus 10 percent tolerance.

Frequency is 50 or 60 hertz, with plus or minus 3 hertz tolerance.

The power quality table sets expectations for power factor and total harmonic distortion. Below 20 percent load, power factor must be greater than zero point nine five. At 20 percent load and above, it must be greater than zero point nine nine. Total harmonic distortion tightens as load rises: less than 15 percent from 5 to 15 percent load, less than 10 percent from 15 to 30 percent load, and less than 5 percent above 30 percent load.

These input requirements matter because the sidecar is not isolated from facility behavior. It has to be a good electrical citizen upstream, not just a useful DC source downstream.
