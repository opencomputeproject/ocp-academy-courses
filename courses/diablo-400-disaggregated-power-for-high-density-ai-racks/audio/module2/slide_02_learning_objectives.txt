By the end of this module, you'll be able to explain the basic Diablo 400 power path from AC input to DC output.

You'll be able to describe the physical rack envelope and the serviceability assumptions that shape the sidecar design. You'll know how the AC power distribution unit, or PDU, feeds the AC to DC power supply modules, and why the specification supports both single feed and dual-feed N plus N redundancy.

You'll also be able to distinguish holdup, ride-through, battery backup units, and capacitor bank units. Those terms often get compressed into a single idea of backup power, but the specification treats them differently.

Finally, you'll be able to recognize the most important output requirements: the plus-minus 400 volts DC model, the rack power capability range, the dynamic load behavior, and the end-to-end efficiency targets.
