# slide 3.2 — objectives
# target: ~60s · ~140 words
# pronunciation: MRC -> "M R C"; APIs -> "A P Is"

By the end of this module you'll be able to do four things.

First, describe the two-tier multi-plane topology that M R C enables and explain why it's lower cost, lower power, and more resilient than a conventional three-tier eight-hundred-gig design.

Second, cite the production-scale evidence — what happens when an optical transceiver flaps, what happens when a tier-one switch reboots, and how the training job behaves through both.

Third, name the headline performance numbers — point-to-point bandwidth, N C C L scale, M R C versus rocky under loss and incast — and what they imply for collective communication.

Fourth, identify the two A P Is that integrators and operators interact with — the application A P I and the controller A P I — and list the main trade-offs and limitations of M R C compared to standard R C.
