# slide 1.7 — content_grid "Who Built MRC and Where to Find It"
# target: ~95s · ~230 words
# pronunciation: MRC -> "M R C"; OCP -> "O C P"; OpenAI -> "Open A I"; NVIDIA -> "Nvidia"; AMD -> "A M D"

M R C is a joint contribution from six companies. Three NIC vendors — AMD, Broadcom, and Nvidia. One C P U and accelerator vendor — Intel. And two hyperscalers — Microsoft and Open A I. They worked together for roughly two years before publishing the version one point zero specification in March twenty twenty-six.

The published spec is seventy-six pages, uses R F C two-one-one-nine normative language, and is released under the Open Web Foundation Final Specification Agreement through O C P. That means any implementer — switch vendor, NIC vendor, software stack, or operator — can build to it without a license fee.

It is already in production. M R C is deployed across all of Open A I's largest Nvidia G B two-hundred supercomputers, including the Stargate site that Oracle Cloud Infrastructure operates in Abilene, Texas, and inside Microsoft's Fairwater data centres. The hardware ecosystem already includes Nvidia ConnectX-Eight and Spectrum-X, AMD Pollara, and Broadcom Thor Ultra and Tomahawk Five, with switch software contributions from Arista and sonic.

Two documents will repay close reading. The specification itself, at opencompute dot org. And the companion paper, "Resilient AI Supercomputer Networking using M R C and S Rv six," which carries the production measurements. Open A I also publishes a high-level overview of the design that's well worth your time. Both links are on the screen.
