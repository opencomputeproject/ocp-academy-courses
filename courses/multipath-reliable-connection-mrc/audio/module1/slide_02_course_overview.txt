# slide 1.2 — course_overview
# target: ~75s · ~180 words
# pronunciation: MRC -> "M R C"

This course is built for data center network engineers and runs about thirty minutes. It's structured in three modules. Module one — the one you're in now — sets the scene. Why does an AI back-end network need a different transport than the ones we already have. What goes wrong at scale. What M R C is at a glance. And who put it together.

Module two goes inside the protocol. We'll walk through the M R C stack, multipath spraying with entropy values, source routing over S R V six, out-of-order receive with direct memory placement, the independent reliability layer of sacks and nacks, the congestion-control story that combines packet trimming with E C N, and the failure-bypass mechanisms that route around broken paths in tens of microseconds.

Module three is about deploying M R C in production — multi-plane topologies, measured performance numbers, the two A P Is you'll touch as an integrator or operator, and the trade-offs and limitations to know about before you commit.

Everything here is grounded in the published specification and the joint paper from the six companies that built it.
