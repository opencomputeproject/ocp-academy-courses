# slide 2.2 — objectives
# target: ~60s · ~145 words
# pronunciation: MRC -> "M R C"; QP -> "Q P"; SRv6 -> "S R V six"; SACK -> "sack"; NACK -> "knack"

By the end of this module you'll be able to do four things.

First, sketch where M R C sits in the stack — what it shares with R C, what it adds on top, what it takes away, and how the on-wire framing looks.

Second, explain how a single queue pair sprays its packets across many paths at once, using entropy values, and how the three supported routing modes — E C M P, structured entropy, and S R V six — actually choose the path.

Third, describe how the receiver places packets directly into memory even when they arrive wildly out of order, and how the independent reliability layer of sacks and knacks tracks delivery.

Fourth, describe how M R C reacts to congestion and how it detects and bypasses a failed path in tens of microseconds — without crashing the connection.
