# pronunciation: DLC -> "D L C"; CDU -> "C D U"; TCS -> "T C S"; FWS -> "F W S"; QD -> "Q D"
By the end of this module, you will be able to describe the basic two-phase D L C loop without treating it like a black box.

You will distinguish sensible heat transfer from latent heat transfer, and explain why boiling can carry more heat with less liquid flow.

You will identify the major loop elements: cold plate, liquid feed, vapor return, condenser, receiver or buffer, optional pump, and C D U controls.

You will explain why saturation pressure is tied to boiling temperature, and why pressure control becomes part of thermal control.

You will also recognize design concerns that do not disappear just because the flow is lower: pressure loss, vapor line sizing, subcooling at the pump inlet, Q D compatibility, manifolds, and reusable infrastructure.

The key idea is simple: two-phase can simplify some operating burdens, but only when the loop is designed deliberately.
