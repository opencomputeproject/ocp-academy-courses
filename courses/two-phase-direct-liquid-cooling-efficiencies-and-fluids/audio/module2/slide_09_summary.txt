# pronunciation: DLC -> "D L C"; TCS -> "T C S"; FWS -> "F W S"; CDU -> "C D U"
Module two connected the physics to the architecture.

Single-phase cooling carries sensible heat as a temperature rise in liquid. Two-phase cooling carries much of the heat through boiling and condensation. That shift can reduce liquid flow, improve hot-spot handling, and change the control problem.

The cold plate, liquid feed, vapor return, condenser, buffer, and optional pump all have to work as a system. Pressure is not just a safety variable. Because saturation pressure and boiling temperature are linked, pressure control is part of temperature control.

The C D U is the boundary where two-phase behavior meets familiar facility heat rejection. Inside the T C S, phase, pressure, charge, line sizing, and compatibility matter. On the F W S side, the facility still manages load and temperature.

Finally, interoperability turns the design from a one-off project into something operators can scale. Components, materials, connectors, manifolds, and records need to prove they belong together.
