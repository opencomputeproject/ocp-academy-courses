# pronunciation: SDS -> "S D S"; PPE -> "P P E"; COA -> "C O A"
Fluid safety starts before anyone opens a container.

The OCP guideline says to review the Safety Data Sheet, or S D S, for the specific fluid before work begins. The S D S gives the hazard information, handling precautions, storage requirements, first-aid measures, and disposal guidance that belong to that material.

Personnel should use appropriate personal protective equipment, or P P E. The guideline names safety glasses or goggles, nitrile or other impervious gloves, and suitable protective clothing to minimize exposure.

Startup also has a documentation step. Vendors are required to provide a Certificate of Analysis, or C O A, for manufactured products to verify product quality. A copy should be retained for records.

Handling discipline matters during installation as well. Components should remain clean and factory sealed until installation. Teams should look for mechanical defects, bent tubes, cracked parts, broken connectors, or contamination before fluid moves.

These steps may sound routine, but they are what make a new cooling technology operationally boring in the best sense. The fluid is part of a controlled system, not an open bucket of coolant.
