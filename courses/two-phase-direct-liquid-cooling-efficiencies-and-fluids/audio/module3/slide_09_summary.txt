Module three turns the fluid into an engineering decision.

The OCP guideline defines the expected property envelope: low toxicity, non-flammability, low G W P, near-zero O D P, the correct boiling-point range, freezing-point expectations, and thermal property limits.

Compatibility depends on the actual wetted-material list. If the fluid touches it, it belongs in the compatibility review. If the material changes, the compatibility basis may need to be re-evaluated.

Testing should move from materials to components to monitored system operation. The seventy-two-hour Soxhlet test is a useful screen, but it is not the whole qualification story.

Safety is equally practical. Review the S D S, use the correct P P E, keep components clean, retain the C O A, and follow supplier and regulatory guidance.

The theme is evidence. A dielectric fluid can reduce some water-loop concerns, but only if the team can prove the fluid, materials, system design, and lifecycle controls fit together.
