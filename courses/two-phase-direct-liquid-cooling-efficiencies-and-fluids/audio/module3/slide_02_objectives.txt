# pronunciation: ODP -> "O D P"; GWP -> "G W P"; SDS -> "S D S"; PPE -> "P P E"; COA -> "C O A"
By the end of this module, you will be able to evaluate the fluid side of a two-phase deployment with more confidence.

You will identify the property criteria in the OCP guideline, including low toxicity, non-flammability, low global warming potential, zero ozone depletion potential, and the required boiling-point range.

You will explain why dielectric fluid compatibility is not generic. It depends on the exact wetted-material list and the specific fluid.

You will describe the evidence path from material identification, to Soxhlet testing, to component testing, to monitored system testing.

You will also recognize the basic safety workflow: review the S D S, use the right P P E, follow handling and storage guidance, and retain quality records such as a C O A.

Finally, you will separate environmental claims from validation. Low O D P and low G W P are important, but they do not replace compatibility testing, leak planning, and responsible disposal.
