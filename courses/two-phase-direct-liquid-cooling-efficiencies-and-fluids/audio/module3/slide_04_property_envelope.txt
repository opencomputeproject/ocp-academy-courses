# pronunciation: ODP -> "O D P"; GWP -> "G W P"; ASHRAE -> "ASH-ray"; A1 -> "A one"
The OCP guideline gives the property envelope for dielectric heat transfer fluids.

Start with safety and phase-change fit. The fluid should have low toxicity, be non-flammable, and fit ASHRAE Standard thirty-four, A one. The corrected boiling-point range is minus twenty-six point three degrees C to sixty degrees C. That minus sign matters.

The environmental targets are near-zero ozone depletion potential, or O D P, and global warming potential, or G W P, below seven hundred.

The remaining limits set thermal and handling boundaries: freezing below minus one hundred degrees C, vapor thermal conductivity below twenty milliwatts per meter kelvin, and liquid thermal conductivity below eighty milliwatts per meter kelvin.

These numbers are the starting screen. The deployment still needs compatibility, stability, handling, leak response, and performance evidence.
