# pronunciation: DLC -> "D L C"; TCS -> "T C S"; FWS -> "F W S"; CDU -> "C D U"
Module one gives us the frame for the rest of the course.

High-density AI infrastructure is changing the cooling problem. More power in less space means airflow, liquid flow, water chemistry, and heat rejection all deserve a fresh look.

Two-phase D L C is not a universal replacement for air, rear-door heat exchangers, single-phase D L C, or immersion. It is a tool for cases where heat flux, rack density, water placement, pumping burden, or site constraints make phase-change cooling valuable.

The loop boundary is essential. The T C S and C D U carry most of the two-phase complexity. The F W S can often continue to behave like a familiar heat-rejection system.

Next, we move from the adoption frame to the architecture itself: how boiling, condensation, pressure control, flow rate, line sizing, and interoperability shape a working two-phase design.
