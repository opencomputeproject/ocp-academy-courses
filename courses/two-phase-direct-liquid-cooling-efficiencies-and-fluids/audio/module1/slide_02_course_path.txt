# pronunciation: DLC -> "D L C"; TCS -> "T C S"; FWS -> "F W S"
The course follows the same path a deployment team would take.

First, we frame the problem. High-density AI infrastructure changes the thermal assumptions that made air cooling, rear-door heat exchangers, and single-phase D L C practical for many years.

Next, we look inside the two-phase loop. You will see where the technology cooling system, or T C S, differs from a single-phase loop, and why the facility water system, or F W S, can often stay familiar.

Then we focus on the fluid. Dielectric heat transfer fluid is not just a coolant. It is a safety, compatibility, monitoring, and lifecycle choice.

Finally, we turn those ideas into an operating playbook: startup, filling, purging, monitoring, leak response, records, and deployment patterns.

Each module ends with a knowledge check. You can retry, and you can continue after an attempt, but the goal is to test the mental model before moving on.
