Filling is not only about getting liquid into the loop. It is about preserving thermal performance over time.

During installation, the liquid filling process should be systematic so the loop avoids trapped air and distributes fluid uniformly. The system should be properly primed before flow begins.

Later, operations can introduce new air. The OCP guideline notes that servers may be replaced or added to the rack over time. Each new server can introduce a small volume of air into the cooling system. Those non-condensable gases can accumulate.

Once the accumulation exceeds a threshold, the system should trigger a non-critical alarm indicating that purging is required. The purge process can use a dedicated purge system connected to a purge port located on the buffer.

The important operating detail is continuity. The guideline states that the system does not need to be shut down during purging. Non-condensable gases can be removed while operation continues.

This matters because non-condensables interfere with condensation and heat transfer. A loop that looks full can still perform poorly if vapor-space composition is wrong. Purging keeps the phase-change behavior aligned with the design.
