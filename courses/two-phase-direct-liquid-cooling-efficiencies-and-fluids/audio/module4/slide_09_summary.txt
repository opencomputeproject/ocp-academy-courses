# pronunciation: DLC -> "D L C"; COA -> "C O A"; SDS -> "S D S"
Module four turns two-phase D L C into an operating checklist.

Start with readiness. Retain the C O A, review safety requirements, verify ambient conditions, inspect hardware, and keep components clean until installation.

Fill the system deliberately, then monitor for the conditions that matter. Vapor pressure, vapor temperature, liquid pressure, liquid temperature, and buffer liquid level are the core signals.

Purge when non-condensable gases move the system away from expected pressure-temperature behavior. The guideline defines a greater-than-twenty-five-percent vapor-pressure deviation as a condition requiring purge.

Maintain the system with the fluid's behavior in mind. Minimal filtration may be enough in normal operation, but loading and cleaning still need protection from contamination.

Respond to leaks without panic and without neglect. Dielectric fluid changes the immediate electronics risk, but the operator still needs detection, repair, S D S guidance, records, and responsible recovery.

Finally, keep lifecycle records complete. That is what makes reuse, troubleshooting, and future IT refreshes auditable.
