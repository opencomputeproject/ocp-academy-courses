# pronunciation: COA -> "C O A"; TCS -> "T C S"
Startup is the moment to catch problems while they are still cheap.

The OCP guideline starts with product quality. Vendors are required to provide a Certificate of Analysis, or C O A, for manufactured products, and the owner should retain a copy for records.

Safety and operational checks come next. The team should review relevant safety requirements, rules, regulations, recommendations, and servicing instructions before operation. If the system includes a refrigerant buffer, the buffer should be fully charged, and any additional fluid should follow the prescribed servicing method.

Environmental conditions matter too. The guideline calls for verifying that ambient temperature remains within the specified range of five degrees C to thirty-five degrees C for proper system function.

Hardware inspection is practical and visual. Look for bent tubes, cracks, broken connectors, or other mechanical defects before fluid moves.

Cleanliness is just as important. Products in the T C S loop should comply with the wetted-material list, remain clean, and stay factory sealed until installation. If there is evidence of damage or contamination, the owner should be notified immediately.

The startup mindset is simple: verify the fluid, verify the parts, verify the environment, and verify cleanliness before the system becomes harder to inspect.
