# pronunciation: OCP -> "O C P"
The monitoring set tells operators whether the phase-change loop is behaving as designed.

The OCP guideline calls for routine monitoring of five signals: vapor pressure, vapor temperature, liquid pressure, liquid temperature, and buffer liquid level.

Each signal answers a practical question. Vapor pressure and temperature show whether the saturated side matches the expected boiling condition. Liquid pressure and temperature show whether supply flow, pump conditions, and inlet temperature are stable. Buffer level shows whether the fluid charge is where it should be.

The key action threshold is vapor pressure. If actual vapor pressure differs from the theoretical vapor pressure for that boiling temperature by more than twenty-five percent, purging is required. The likely cause is excess non-condensable gas or another anomaly.

That turns monitoring into an operating rule: compare, alarm, purge, and confirm the loop has returned to its intended state.

Good monitoring turns two-phase operation into a managed control problem instead of a mystery.
