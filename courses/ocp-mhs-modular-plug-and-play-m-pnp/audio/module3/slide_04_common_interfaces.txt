# pronunciation: HPM -> "H P M"; FPGA -> "F P G A"; SCM -> "S C M"; BMC -> "B M C"; DC-SCM -> "D C S C M"; LTPI -> "L T P I"; SGPIO -> "S G P I O"; SPI -> "S P I"; I2C -> "I two C"; I3C -> "I three C"; JTAG -> "J TAG"; M-PESTI -> "M PESTI"; M-XIO -> "M X I O"; M-PIC -> "M PIC"; DSSI -> "D S S I"; M-CRPS -> "M C R P S"; MXIO -> "M X I O"; IO -> "I O"; M-PNP -> "M P N P"

The HPM FPGA sits at a crossroads. Look outward toward the SCM — toward the BMC — and the FPGA exposes a defined set of interfaces specified in DC-SCM two point one: LTPI, SGPIO, SPI, and basic I2C or I3C. The spec requires that the FPGA support at least one of LTPI or SGPIO; both are allowed. LTPI carries virtual wires and a memory-mapped data channel over a serial link. SGPIO carries virtual wires only — memory-mapped access in that case goes over a separate SPI or I2C-I3C path. JTAG is also defined for FPGA programming and discovery. Look inward toward the HPM and the FPGA exposes M-PESTI for tunneling sideband signals to risers and backplanes, M-XIO FlexIO for high-speed extensible IO, M-PIC sideband for platform infrastructure connectivity, and DSSI for M-CRPS power supplies. There's also an SGPIO link directly to the power distribution board for board management. The HPM FPGA shall support virtual wire access plus memory-mapped access using one of two combinations: SGPIO for virtual wires combined with SPI or I2C-I3C for memory map, or LTPI for both. That's a normative requirement out of section eight of the spec, and it's the single most important constraint on FPGA design for M-PNP conformance. Everything else is built on top of the assumption that the BMC has both random-access reads and virtual-wire transport into the FPGA.
