By the end of this module you'll be able to do six things. One: explain the standardization concept — what's locked, what's a vendor's call, and where the system integrator can extend. Two: enumerate the common interfaces between the HPM FPGA and the rest of the platform. Three: list the required and optional IP blocks in the catalog and what each one does. Four: describe how the FPGA memory map is organized and how the BMC accesses it. Five: walk the ten power states, with particular attention to the four hold-off states that gate attestation. Six: explain the role of the virtual wires framework and the FPGA self-describing JSON. Six big topics in nine content slides. Let's begin with the concept.
