# pronunciation: HPM -> "H P M"; BMC -> "B M C"; FPGA -> "F P G A"; SCM -> "S C M"; Fru -> "Fru" (read as the word, not letters); PDB -> "P D B"; PDBs -> "P D Bs"

Discovery is breadth-first. The BMC starts at the center of the system and spreads outward, one ring at a time, only descending into a peripheral after every peer at the current ring has been identified. Four phases. Phase zero: SCM power and initialization. Coin-cell battery power flows; when the DC-SCM is fully seated, the HPM eFuse enables pre-standby power; the SCM's auxiliary regulators come up, including the rail that feeds the HPM's Fru EEPROM. The SCM BMC boots, optionally validates itself against a silicon root of trust, and initializes. Phase one: HPM discovery. The zone expands to the HPM board — its Fru, its FPGA, its on-board components. Phase two: non-endpoint peripherals. Risers, backplanes, power distribution boards, control panels — the intermediate boards that carry endpoints but aren't endpoints themselves. Phase three: endpoint peripherals. NICs, drives, OCP CEM cards, M-CRPS power supplies. The reason for breadth-first is dependency: each ring's JSON description tells the BMC what to expect on the next ring. The riser's JSON tells the BMC which PCIe lanes route to which slots; only after that does it make sense to enumerate the cards in those slots. Boot is partially interleaved with this — but most of discovery completes before the Bios gets a chance to run.
