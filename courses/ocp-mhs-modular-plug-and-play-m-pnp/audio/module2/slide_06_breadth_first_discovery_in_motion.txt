Breadth-first discovery gives the B M C a deterministic way to expand its view of the platform. Phase zero starts with the secure control module and the resources that are available before the rest of the system is powered.

Phase one identifies the H P M and retrieves the information that describes its local resources. That description points outward to the next ring. Phase two covers non-endpoint peripherals such as risers, backplanes, power distribution boards, and control panels. Their descriptions expose the paths to the final ring. Phase three reaches endpoint peripherals, including Nicks, drives, add-in cards, and power supplies.

The important pattern is dependency. Each ring provides enough trusted description to find and interpret the next one. The BMC doesn't guess the topology or jump directly to endpoints. It builds the platform model outward, one known layer at a time.
