# pronunciation: Fru -> "Fru" (read as the word, not letters); HPM -> "H P M"; BMC -> "B M C"; Bios -> "BYE-oss" (mixed case so it reads as a word); JSON -> "Jason"; MMBI -> "M M B I"

By the end of this module you'll be able to do five things. One: explain the three pillars of the specification — Fru information, discovery, and boot — and how they fit together. Two: distinguish between local attributes stored on the component itself and extended attributes stored elsewhere, and know where each lives in memory. Three: trace the breadth-first discovery process through its four phases, from SCM initialization all the way to endpoint peripherals. Four: walk through HPM discovery with security gates, calling out the exact power states the BMC and FPGA are in at each step. And five: identify the role of MMBI in the BMC-to-Bios handoff during early boot. We've got nine content slides to cover this. Let's start with the pillars.
