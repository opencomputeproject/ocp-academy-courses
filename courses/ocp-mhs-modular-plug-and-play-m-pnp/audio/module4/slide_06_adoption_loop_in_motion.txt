M-PNP adoption works as a loop, not as a one-way handoff from specification writers to implementers. Schemas define the shape of the self-describing data. Worked examples turn that shape into concrete H P M, FPGA, riser, backplane, and power-board descriptions that engineers can inspect.

Tooling then turns those descriptions into usable artifacts. frupack dot pie packages JSON into binary Fru data today. FPGA-to-JSON generation is the planned next step, alongside validators and ingestion libraries the community still needs.

Implementations are where the specifications meet real hardware. Prototype B M C, Bios, FPGA, and peripheral combinations reveal missing fields, ambiguous requirements, and conformance questions. Those findings return to the workstream as issues, proposed text, examples, and code. The next schema or specification revision carries those lessons forward. That feedback cycle is how the bundle moves from version zero point five toward a stable version one point zero ecosystem.
