For this course, the main single-phase fluid families are treated water and P G twenty-five. Treated water can deliver excellent heat transfer, but it needs active attention to corrosion, biological growth, conductivity, solids, hardness, and additives.

P G twenty-five means a twenty-five percent propylene glycol fluid. It gives up some thermal performance compared with water, but it adds biostatic margin when the concentration stays at or above twenty-five percent. That is one reason P G twenty-five has become a common single-phase direct-to-chip path.

Two-phase dielectric refrigerants appear here only as a contrast, because their operating logic is different.
