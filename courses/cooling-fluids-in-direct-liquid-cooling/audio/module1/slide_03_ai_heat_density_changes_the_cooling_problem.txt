A I racks changed the thermal conversation because heat is now concentrated where air cooling struggles to keep up. The trend is clear: accelerator power is rising, more accelerators are packed into smaller footprints, and rack power is moving from familiar tens of kilowatts into hundreds of kilowatts.

That does not mean air disappears. In many direct-to-chip deployments, liquid removes most of the heat while air still handles residual loads. But it does mean the fluid becomes part of the reliability story. If the fluid fouls, corrodes, grows biology, or plugs microchannels, the compute business case can become a maintenance problem very quickly.
