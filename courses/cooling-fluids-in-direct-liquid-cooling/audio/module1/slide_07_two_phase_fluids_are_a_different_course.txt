This is the one two-phase slide in the course because the technical overlap is limited. The shared lesson is governance: know the fluid, know the wetted materials, keep records, and use the right specification.

The fluid behavior is different. Two-phase D L C uses low-boiling-point dielectric refrigerants such as R five fifteen B, R twelve thirty-three Z D E, and R thirteen thirty-six M Z Z Z. Their advantage is latent heat during boiling or evaporation. As single-phase liquids, these fluids have low specific heat. Running them without phase change would waste their main advantage and require much higher flow rates.

For two-phase details, use the linked OCP guideline and the separate two-phase D L C course.
