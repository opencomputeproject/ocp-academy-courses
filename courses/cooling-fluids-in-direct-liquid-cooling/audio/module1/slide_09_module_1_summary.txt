Module one set the boundary. The course is about single-phase fluid decisions inside the T C S loop, not the facility water loop and not a full two-phase refrigerant course.

The reason this matters is straightforward: higher-density A I racks turn fluid health into an availability issue. The useful questions are practical ones. Is the fluid compatible? How is it filled? How is it filtered? What is monitored? Who decides when to adjust it?

The common ground with two-phase systems is mostly governance. The operating guidance diverges quickly, so the rest of the course stays with treated water and P G twenty-five.
