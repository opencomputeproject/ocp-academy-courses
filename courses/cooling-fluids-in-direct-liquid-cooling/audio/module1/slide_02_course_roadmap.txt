This course has four modules. First, we set the boundary: where the fluid lives, why high-density A I makes the decision matter, and why two-phase refrigerants belong in a separate learning path.

Second, we compare treated water and P G twenty-five, then unpack the property targets, inhibitor chemistry, and compatibility evidence behind a fluid specification.

Third, we move into commissioning: serviceable design, leak checks, cleaning, filling, startup sampling, filtration, and microbial control.

Finally, we cover steady-state operations: routine monitoring, lab testing, trend interpretation, supplier-guided adjustments, fluid mixing, useful life, disposal, and the records that let the infrastructure survive refresh cycles.
