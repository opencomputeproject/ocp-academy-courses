Hard-water ions such as calcium and magnesium can enter the loop through poor water quality or by leaching from materials such as hose fillers. Once present, they can interact with inhibitor chemistry and form precipitates. In the wrong place, those precipitates become scale, filter loading, reduced heat transfer, or microchannel blockage.

Hard-water stability examples are useful because they connect lab observation to operating risk. The point is not to turn learners into test chemists. The point is to show why compatibility, water quality, inhibitor chemistry, and filtration belong in the same conversation.
