Module two moved from product names to specification thinking. Treated water and P G twenty-five both have a place, but they carry different operating burdens. Water has excellent thermal behavior. P G twenty-five adds biostatic margin when concentration is maintained.

The fluid quality table gives measurable signals for the loop: solids, dissolved material, conductivity, pH, hardness, turbidity, bacteria, and inhibitor levels. Inhibitor chemistry matters because it shapes corrosion protection and deposit risk. Compatibility matters because the fluid touches more than the cold plate.

A good single-phase fluid specification is evidence-backed, system-specific, and designed to be operated.
