Compatibility follows every surface the fluid touches. The draft guidance describes wetted materials as the collective set of materials in contact with the fluid. That includes obvious items such as cold plates and heat exchangers, but also pump seals, quick disconnect seals, hoses, filter media, reservoirs, thread sealants, brazing materials, and plated surfaces.

The important rule is conservative: the heat transfer fluid specification is tied closely to the wetted material list. Any addition to that list requires re-evaluation. A compatible loop is not just a compatible metal. It is a compatible system under real temperature, pressure, and service conditions.
