Corrosion inhibitors are one reason a coolant is not just water in a pipe. Inorganic additive technology, or I A T, uses chemistries such as phosphate, silicate, and borate to form protective films quickly. The tradeoff is shorter inhibitor life and greater sensitivity to some impurities.

Organic acid technology, or O A T, was developed for longer-life protection. Hybrid O A T combines the longer-life organic acid approach with small amounts of inorganic chemistry for faster protection.

For data center operators, the lesson is not that one acronym always wins. The lesson is to know which inhibitor system you are using, what materials it protects, how stable it is with contaminants, and how the supplier expects you to monitor it.
