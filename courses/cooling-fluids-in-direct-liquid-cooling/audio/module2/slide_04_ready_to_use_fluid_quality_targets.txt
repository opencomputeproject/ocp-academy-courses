The draft single-phase guidance turns fluid quality into measurable targets. These are not decorative numbers. They are early warning indicators for corrosion, deposits, microbial activity, contamination, and loss of inhibitor protection.

A few examples show the pattern. Conductivity and T D S help indicate dissolved material and possible contamination. Hardness points to makeup water quality or leaching sources. Turbidity and T S S point to particles that can load filters or threaten microchannels. Bacteria counts show biological risk. Azoles indicate protection for copper and yellow metals.

The learner does not need to memorize every threshold today. The important habit is to compare each loop against its baseline and treatment plan.
