Temperature changes chemistry and compatibility. The draft guidance says typical T C S operating temperature is below forty-nine degrees C, or one hundred twenty degrees F, and is not expected to exceed sixty-six degrees C, or one hundred fifty degrees F.

That limit is not just a thermal comfort statement. Operating above that range requires review of the wetted materials list and the treatment chemistry, because elastomers, plastics, plating, inhibitors, and corrosion behavior can all respond differently at higher temperature. Stagnation temperature at the cold plate surface also matters, because the surface can see a harsher local condition than the bulk loop.
