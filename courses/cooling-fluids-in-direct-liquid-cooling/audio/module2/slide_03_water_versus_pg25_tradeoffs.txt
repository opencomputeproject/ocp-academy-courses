Water is an excellent heat transfer fluid. If the only question were heat capacity, water would be hard to beat. But in a data center T C S loop, heat transfer is not the only question. Water must be managed for corrosion, biological growth, dissolved and suspended solids, pH, hardness, and additive balance.

P G twenty-five adds propylene glycol, primarily for biostatic behavior in this context. The operational tradeoff is plain: if the propylene glycol concentration stays at or above twenty-five percent, the system can reduce the need for biocides compared with treated water. The price is lower thermal performance and the need to maintain concentration.
