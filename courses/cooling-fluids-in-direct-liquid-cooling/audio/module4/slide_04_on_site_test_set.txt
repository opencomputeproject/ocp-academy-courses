The routine on-site test set is the operator's first line of visibility. Conductivity, pH, hardness, bacteria, azoles, turbidity, other inhibitors, appearance, and filter loading each tell a different part of the story.

Conductivity is easy to measure and can indicate contamination or dissolved material. pH helps show whether the chemistry window is stable. Hardness can point to poor makeup water or leaching. Bacteria and turbidity point to biological and particulate risk. Azoles and other inhibitors show whether the corrosion protection package remains in range. Appearance and filter loading add practical operator signals that should not be ignored.
