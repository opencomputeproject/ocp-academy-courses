Monitoring starts immediately after initial fill and circulation. The draft guidance then recommends quarterly routine testing, with more frequent retesting, such as weekly checks, whenever a parameter is out of specification until it returns to the normal range.

Independent laboratory quality assurance is also recommended quarterly for one year. If results are stable, the lab frequency can drop to every six months after that first year. The exact rigor should scale with system size and criticality. Larger or more critical systems may justify automated monitoring and more frequent review.

The schedule is not the goal. The goal is catching drift early enough to act deliberately.
