Laboratory quality assurance adds depth that routine field checks cannot provide. The draft guidance calls for independent lab analysis approved by the owner, including I C P analysis to document water quality and changes over time.

The laboratory view should include total suspended solids, total dissolved solids, filtered and unfiltered corrosion byproducts, and metals such as copper, iron, zinc, chromium, and nickel. Corrosion coupons may be used in some cases, but the guidance warns that conventional coupons or probes may not represent the high-temperature, finned geometry inside cold plates. That makes chemistry trend data especially valuable.
