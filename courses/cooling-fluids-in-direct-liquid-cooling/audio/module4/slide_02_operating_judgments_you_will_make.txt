By the end of this module, you will be able to describe the routine monitoring schedule and identify which checks belong on site versus in an independent laboratory.

You will be able to interpret trends instead of reacting to one isolated number. That includes conductivity rise, metal-ion drift, hardness, bacteria, turbidity, filter loading, and inhibitor depletion.

You will also be able to explain why adjustments should be supplier-guided, why fluid mixing complicates monitoring, and which records must be maintained so future operators can understand the loop they inherit.
