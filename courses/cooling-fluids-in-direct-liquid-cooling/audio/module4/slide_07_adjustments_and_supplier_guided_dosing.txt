Adjustments should be controlled because the cure can become the contaminant. The draft guidance says adjustments should only be made under guidance from the heat transfer fluid supplier and according to the approved treatment plan.

Before dosing, the system volume must be known. Add the predetermined amount of inhibitor or additive while the heat transfer fluid is circulating. Circulate for thirty minutes, then collect a representative follow-up sample. If results still do not align with the specification, consult technical support.

Documentation matters here. Record the date, system, additive type, amount added, and the analytical reports before and after the adjustment.
