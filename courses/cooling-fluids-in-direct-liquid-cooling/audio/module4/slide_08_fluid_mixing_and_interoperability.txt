Fluid mixing should be avoided where possible, but operations create a practical reality: some mixing events are likely. An O D M may ship equipment with flush residue. A colocation site may face different customer fluids. A supply-chain issue may require emergency top-off. An operator may simply make a mistake.

The important guidance is not a blanket category rule. Do not assume all O A T fluids can mix, or all I A T and O A T fluids cannot mix. The speaker emphasized that mixing must be evaluated fluid by fluid. Even when a specific mix passes storage or corrosion tests, it can complicate in-service monitoring because baseline inhibitor targets and corrective actions become ambiguous.
