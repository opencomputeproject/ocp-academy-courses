Module four turned fluid health into an operating model. Routine checks give fast visibility. Laboratory analysis gives deeper evidence. Automated monitoring can help larger or more critical systems catch drift earlier. But the real power is trend interpretation against a clean baseline.

Adjustments should be supplier-guided, volume-based, mixed, retested, and documented. Fluid mixing should be avoided when possible and evaluated fluid by fluid when it happens. Useful life, disposal, and refresh cycles all depend on records.

The final lesson is plain: reliable single-phase D L C is not set and forget. It is monitor, interpret, correct, and document.
