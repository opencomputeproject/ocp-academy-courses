A single sample can tell you a value. A trend tells you direction, speed, and likely cause. That distinction matters because several parameters are only meaningful against a baseline.

A conductivity value may be acceptable today, but a rapid rise can signal contamination. Copper below an action limit may still matter if it is climbing steadily from a clean baseline. Hardness can reveal poor makeup water quality or material leaching. Turbidity can point to particles before a filter plugs. Thermal performance drift can be a general indicator of cold plate cleanliness, even when the exact cause still needs investigation.

Good operations connect the data, not just the table cells.
