Module three is about the field practices that protect the loop after the fluid has been specified. A strong fluid choice can still fail if the system is dirty, the flush is careless, the leak-check fluid is incompatible, or the first sample is never captured.

The message is practical. Design the loop so it can be serviced. Fill it cleanly. Filter where service teams can act. Control microbial risk. Then preserve the startup baseline, because that baseline is what makes later monitoring meaningful.
