Water-based heat transfer fluid can support microbial growth. In cold plate systems, that matters because biofouling can accumulate in microchannels and degrade thermal performance. The draft guidance recommends operational total viable bacteria below one hundred colony-forming units per milliliter, with biocide treatment highly recommended above one thousand.

The biocide choice also matters. Non-oxidizing biocides may be used if needed, following the label. Halogens such as chlorine should not be used because they create corrosion risk. Frequent biocide addition can also build organics or other contaminants, eventually requiring a flush. Biological control is a balance, not a blank check to dose more chemistry.
