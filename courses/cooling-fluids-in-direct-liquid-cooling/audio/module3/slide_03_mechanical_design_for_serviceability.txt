Fluid maintenance is much easier when serviceability is designed in. The draft guidance calls for ports to drain and fill heat transfer fluid and to inject chemicals while the T C S loop remains operational. It also points to feeders or reservoir and injection-pump arrangements, with wetted feeder materials that comply with the same compatibility expectations.

Complete drainage matters because deadlegs can trap contaminants or microbial populations. The same logic applies to reservoirs, valves, air vents, gauges, and isolation points. They are not extras. They determine whether the team can make controlled corrections without turning routine maintenance into downtime.
