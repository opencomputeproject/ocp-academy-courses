Module three focused on the field practices that protect the fluid and the hardware. Serviceability starts in mechanical design. The loop needs drain, fill, sample, isolation, and chemical-addition paths that are compatible with the same wetted-material rules as the rest of the system.

Clean startup matters because cold plate microchannels are not a good place to discover construction debris. Leak checks, flushes, final fill, and baseline sampling all need documented procedures. Filtration and microbial control then become ongoing defenses.

The simple takeaway is that many later fluid problems are really commissioning or cleanliness problems with a delayed symptom.
