Startup is the moment to create the baseline that operations will use later. After the final system startup, with all components in line and the specified heat transfer fluid in place, the guidance calls for bleeding air if necessary and checking for leaks while circulating for fifteen minutes.

Then take a representative sample from each T C S loop and retain the record for the life of the fluid. Label the sample with the unit, fluid identity, and date. This sounds administrative, but it is technical. Without a clean baseline, later conductivity, metal-ion, bacteria, turbidity, or inhibitor readings are much harder to interpret.
