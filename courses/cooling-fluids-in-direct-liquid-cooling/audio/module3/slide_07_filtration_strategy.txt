Filtration is the practical defense between routine particles and expensive cold plate cleaning. The draft recommends sidestream filtration below five microns to minimize biofoulants trapped inside cold plate microchannels. It also notes that the system should filter less than ten percent of the heat transfer fluid flow rate without affecting full system flow.

Inline mesh filters can protect heat exchangers and quick connectors, but they are not a substitute for sidestream filtration. The Project Deschutes example is a useful anchor: four twenty-five micron inline filters and one zero point two micron sidestream filter. The broader lesson is to put the filtration burden where maintenance teams can inspect and service it.
