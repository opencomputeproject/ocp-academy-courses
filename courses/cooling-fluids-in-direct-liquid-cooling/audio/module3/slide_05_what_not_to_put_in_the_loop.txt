Commissioning can create fluid problems if teams reach for familiar cleaning habits without checking chemistry. The draft guidance says to avoid chemical cleaners and detergents unless the heat transfer fluid supplier recommends them. Remnants can be corrosive or incompatible with the final fluid treatment.

It also calls out silicate-based cleaners. Residual silicate can precipitate with pH shift and create deposition within cold plate microchannels. That is exactly the kind of hidden risk this course is trying to prevent.

The operating principle is simple: if an additive is not in the treatment plan, it does not belong in the loop without supplier approval.
