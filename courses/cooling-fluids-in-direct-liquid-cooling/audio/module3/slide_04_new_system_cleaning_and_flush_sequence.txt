For a new system, the guidance starts with cleanliness. Install new inline filters. Fill with clean, non-corrosive water that meets the specified limits, while cold plates are isolated to avoid construction debris reaching microchannels. Circulate for thirty minutes at room temperature to mix and suspend debris, then drain the liquid.

After that first pass, replace filters if needed and repeat the fill and flush with cold plates connected. Only then move to the final heat transfer fluid fill. The sequence may look basic, but it reflects a hard lesson: debris is easiest to remove before it reaches the most delicate geometry in the loop.
