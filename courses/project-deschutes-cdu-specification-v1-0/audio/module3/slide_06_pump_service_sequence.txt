Serviceability is more than leaving enough floor space. The CDU has to preserve flow, contain coolant, and de-energize the component before a technician breaks a connection.

For a pump swap, begin by transferring duty to the redundant pump and confirming stable secondary-side flow. Then close the supply isolation valve for the pump being serviced. Connect a drain hose to the drain barb at the bottom of the pump, open the drain valve, and use the Schrader valve as needed to clear the line.

Once the branch is isolated and drained, remove the gussets and flange bolts. Disconnect the four-inch Tri-clamp, and unplug the pressure sensor if required. Loosen the Victaulic connections at the controlled break points. Finger-safe connectors, mechanical interlocks, and connector shortpins protect the technician and prevent current flow during disengagement.

After the pump is unbolted, an assistance device supports removal through the specified service envelope. Pump A comes out from the front. Pump B normally comes out from the rear.

The mental model is simple: maintain redundant flow, isolate, drain, de-energize, disconnect, and remove. The specification still requires each vendor to submit its field-replaceable-unit maintenance procedure for customer review.
