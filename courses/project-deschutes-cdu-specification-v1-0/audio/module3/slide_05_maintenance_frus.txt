A two-megawatt CDU is a high-availability resource. The Deschutes spec treats maintenance as a first-class concern, with detailed clearance and procedure requirements in section thirteen.

Front serviceability is the design intent. Both filters — primary and secondary — are front-serviceable, meaning you don't need rear access to swap them. The side-stream microfiltration filter, the expansion tank, and the control panel are also front-serviceable.

Pumps are slightly different. Pump A services from the front. Pump B services from the rear, unless rear access is blocked by a structural pillar — in which case it can be removed from the side. Both pumps have controlled break points at their inlet and outlet connections, isolation valves, drain ports, and connectorized power. The procedure is published, and the spec mandates that vendors propose their own maintenance procedures for any field-replaceable unit, for customer review.

Version one dot zero specifically improved serviceability on two components. The variable-frequency drives gained new clearance accessories. The expansion tank became front-serviceable for the first time. Both changes came directly from prior-generation operational experience.

One important exception: the secondary loop flow meter requires powering off the CDU for service. That's a deliberate trade-off, and operators should plan flow-meter swaps as scheduled events rather than hot swaps.

For tracking and traceability, the spec includes a full barcode labeling standard in section seventeen — every component is labeled with a manufacturer ID, part number, serial number, country of origin, and date code, on a standardized yellow P-M-S eight-zero-three-C background. Spare parts, replacements, and warranty claims all key off these labels.
