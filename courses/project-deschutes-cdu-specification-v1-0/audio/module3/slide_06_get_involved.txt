If you're ready to engage with Project Deschutes, here's where to start.

First, download the version one dot zero specification directly from the Open Compute Project document library. The PDF is the source of truth for every number and procedure we've covered in this course.

Second, visit the Coolant Distribution Unit subproject community page on opencompute dot org. It's the central hub for the working group — including ongoing discussions, contributors, and updates as the spec evolves.

Third, if you're sourcing rather than building, the OCP Marketplace already lists Deschutes-based CDUs from multiple suppliers. The link is filtered directly to the CDU category — so you can compare vendors, lead times, and specifications side by side.

And fourth, if you want broader context on liquid cooling technologies, OCP Academy publishes a four-course primer learning plan called the Liquid Cooling Technologies Overview. We linked the teaser back on slide two of Module one — and the full learning plan is just one click away in your resources panel.

Project Deschutes is a starting point, not a finish line. Future revisions of the spec will incorporate community feedback on manufacturing efficiency, validation procedures, and bill-of-materials refinements. If you have design-for-manufacturing ideas, validation data, or operational lessons to share, the working group wants to hear from you.

To quote Qi Wang at the OCP EMEA Summit: open is the key to scale.
