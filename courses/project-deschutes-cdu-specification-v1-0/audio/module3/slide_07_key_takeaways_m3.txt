Let's lock in the key points from this module.

First, the Project Deschutes specification ships with the Redmond Hot Aisle Containment interface — together they form a complete two-megawatt liquid-cooled deployment template, sized to fit most worldwide colocation data centers.

Second, power and cooling are co-designed. The four-makes-three rack power architecture, eight six-hundred-amp bus ducts per row, and ninety-kilowatt-per-rack-spot capacity work in concert with the CDU's thermal and hydraulic envelope.

Third, the unit is engineered for serviceability. Front-serviceable filters, pumps, expansion tank, and control panel; controlled break points and isolation valves; full barcode traceability; and detailed maintenance procedures in section thirteen of the spec.

Fourth, Google's three-tier supplier model — Approved, Reserved, and Recommended Vendor Lists — is designed to grow an open, competitive ecosystem with ten-to-twelve-week worldwide lead times and competitive pricing.

And fifth, the entire specification is open, evolving, and inviting community input. Future revisions will incorporate design-for-manufacturing feedback, validation refinements, and bill-of-materials improvements contributed back by the industry.
