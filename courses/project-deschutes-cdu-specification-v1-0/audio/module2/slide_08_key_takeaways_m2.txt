Let's lock in what we just covered.

First, the unit's physical envelope is sixty-five by ninety-one by forty-two inches, weighing nearly seven thousand pounds wet. The mechanical design — Victaulic and Tri-clamp fittings, layered filtration, stainless piping with EPDM hoses — is engineered for long-term operability, not just initial commissioning.

Second, hydraulic performance is five hundred gallons per minute at eighty to ninety psi on the IT side, with both two-pump and single-pump performance curves published. Thermal performance holds a three-degree approach at the full two-megawatt load using three parallel dual-pass Alfa Laval heat exchangers, and beats spec across most of the operating envelope.

Third, the core components are named with first-source part numbers — Speck pumps, ABB VFDs, Belimo primary valve, Alfa Laval heat exchangers — giving anyone building to this spec a known starting point.

Fourth, the controls system is a Modbus-over-TCP PLC with full IPv4 and IPv6 dual-stack support, running two default control loops: pump speed for IT differential pressure, and primary-valve position for approach temperature.

And fifth, redundant subcomponents are designed to be hot-swappable in continuous operation, on a dual-feed electrical architecture rated for two-N redundancy.
