Now let's name the core components by part number, because if you're building or sourcing a Deschutes CDU, these are the references the spec puts on the table.

The pump is from Speck, model T-O-E-M-A zero-six-five-two-zero-zero. Each pump is rated for five hundred gallons per minute at one hundred twelve psi, drawing thirty-seven kilowatts. It's a seal-less design, which eliminates the most common failure point in centrifugal pumps for clean-water service. Two pumps operate in N-plus-one redundancy.

Each pump runs on its own variable-frequency drive. The first-source VFD is the ABB ACH-five-eighty, rated at fifty horsepower, in a NEMA Type twelve enclosure. Version one dot zero specifically updated VFD serviceability with new accessories and clearance requirements — another lesson from prior-generation operation.

The primary-side control valve is a Belimo B-six-five-zero-zero — the actively modulated valve that holds the approach temperature stable.

Filtration uses standard cartridges sized for the twenty-five to forty-four-micron primary loop, the five-hundred-micron facility strainer, and the zero-point-two micron side-stream loop.

On the sensor side, the unit publishes a comprehensive telemetry suite. Absolute pressure and temperature are measured on all four CDU manifolds — rack water supply, rack water return, process chill water supply, and process chill water return.

Flow is measured to plus or minus zero-point-five percent of reading. Each pump reports speed in RPM and percent, plus its power consumption.

Leak rope detection covers three zones, integrated with the PLC for alarming. And coolant telemetry includes pH and electrical conductivity, so you can monitor water chemistry without manual sampling.
