Let's look at what the unit actually delivers hydraulically — because the published curves matter more than the headline number.

The hydraulic target on the secondary side is five hundred gallons per minute at eighty to ninety pounds per square inch of available differential pressure. That eighty-to-ninety psi is the pressure head the CDU makes available downstream — enough to drive coolant out to the wall manifolds, through dozens of rack-level cold plates, and back to the unit.

In normal operation, two pumps run together in N-plus-one mode. The spec publishes the full pump curve for two-pump operation with PG-twenty-five coolant on page nineteen, and the single-pump fallback curve on page twenty. The fallback curve is important: it shows that with one pump down, the unit still delivers usable flow at reduced pressure — enough time to schedule a swap rather than perform an emergency outage.

On the facility side, the unit consumes around five hundred gallons per minute at the rated two-megawatt load, with a facility-side differential pressure between fifteen and thirty psi. That's a meaningful design choice — the unit pulls only the facility flow it actually needs, modulated by the primary control valve we discussed in Module one.

The full operating envelope is zero to one hundred thirty psi gauge. A pressure relief valve protects the system at one hundred thirty psi. Quick-disconnect telemetry ports use Parker N-S-P two-five-one fittings with O-R-B six threads — so pressure sensors can be hot-swapped without draining the loop.
