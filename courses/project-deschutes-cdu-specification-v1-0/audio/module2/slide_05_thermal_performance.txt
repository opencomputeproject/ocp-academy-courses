Thermal performance is where the unit's full design intent comes together.

At the rated two-megawatt heat load, with five hundred gallons per minute flowing on both sides of the heat exchangers, the unit holds a three-degree-Celsius approach temperature. Three degrees is a tight target for a CDU at this scale — it's what makes the cold-plate inlet temperatures cold enough to support the highest-density AI silicon.

Inside the unit, three dual-pass liquid-to-liquid heat exchangers operate in parallel. The first-source vendor is Alfa Laval, model C-B two-one-zero, dash, two-seven-six A-H. Three exchangers in parallel reduce pressure drop on both sides of the unit, and they let the system continue meeting thermal targets if one exchanger needs isolation for service.

Page twenty-one of the spec publishes a seven-point operating table that's worth knowing. At the rated two thousand one hundred fifty kilowatt point with five hundred forty-five gallons per minute, the approach temperature is two-point-three degrees Celsius — comfortably below the three-degree spec. At lower loads, approach tightens further: at six hundred forty kilowatts and one hundred sixty-two gallons per minute, it's one-point-six degrees. The unit performs better than the headline number across most of its envelope.

Coolant options on both sides are deionized water — including the ChemTreat, Garratt-Callahan, and Veolia formulations the spec qualifies — or a propylene-glycol-twenty-five mixture. Operating coolant temperature ranges from eighteen to fifty-five degrees Celsius, which covers warm-water cooling deployments common in modern hyperscale.
