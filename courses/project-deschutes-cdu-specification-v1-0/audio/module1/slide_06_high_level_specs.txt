The CDU is rated for a thermal load of two thousand kilowatts — two megawatts — at a three-degree-Celsius approach temperature.

On the IT, or secondary, side, it delivers five hundred gallons per minute, with eighty to ninety pounds per square inch of available differential pressure. That's the head the CDU makes available to drive coolant out to the rack manifolds and back through the cold plates.

On the facility, or primary, side, it consumes five hundred gallons per minute at the rated load — which works out to roughly zero-point-two-five gallons per minute per kilowatt of heat rejected.

The unit operates at zero to one hundred thirty pounds per square inch gauge, with coolant temperatures from eighteen to fifty-five degrees Celsius. Coolant options are deionized water, or a propylene-glycol-twenty-five mixture, often called PG-twenty-five.
