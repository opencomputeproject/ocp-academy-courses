Next-generation AI and machine learning clusters are pushing rack power densities well beyond what traditional air cooling can deliver, both economically and physically.

Direct liquid cooling — abbreviated DLC — solves that problem by removing heat directly at the chip or server level, using cold plates and a dedicated secondary coolant loop. A single DLC rack can support one hundred kilowatts or more of IT load, while consuming dramatically less electricity and floor space than an equivalent air-cooled deployment.

But DLC by itself isn't a complete cooling system. It needs a precise interface between the building's chilled-water plant and the cold plates inside the racks. That interface is the Coolant Distribution Unit, or CDU — a piece of equipment that delivers cold, clean, pressure-controlled coolant to the IT loop, while transferring heat back to the facility loop.

Until now, the industry has been missing a standardized, openly published, high-performance CDU at the scale modern AI clusters demand. Different hyperscalers built different proprietary designs. Suppliers had to retool for each customer. Lead times stretched, costs stayed high, and choice was limited.

Project Deschutes is designed to fix exactly that gap. It targets two megawatts of thermal capacity in a single unit, with industry-leading hydraulic performance and serviceability — and crucially, the entire design, including manufacturing quality and maintenance procedures, is open and available to any company that wants to build or deploy to it.
