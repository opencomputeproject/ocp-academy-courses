Welcome to OCP Academy. This course covers Project Deschutes — the first Coolant Distribution Unit, or CDU, specification ever contributed to the Open Compute Project. The specification we're studying is version one dot zero, effective February twenty-sixth, twenty twenty-six.

Project Deschutes is Google's fifth-generation CDU design, informed by more than seven years of liquid cooling at hyperscale. By contributing it to OCP, Google is inviting the entire industry to build, deploy, and improve on a single open design.

Over the next forty minutes, you'll learn the exact performance targets, the internal architecture, the mechanical and electrical details, the control strategy, and the three-tier supply-chain approach that together enable reliable two-megawatt liquid cooling for the next generation of artificial intelligence and machine learning workloads.

Whether you're a thermal architect, a data center operator, or a procurement lead, this course gives you what you need to evaluate, deploy, or build to this open spec.
