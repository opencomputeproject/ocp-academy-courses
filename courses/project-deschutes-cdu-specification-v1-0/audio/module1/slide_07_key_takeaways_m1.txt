Let's lock in the key points from this module before we open the unit.

First, Project Deschutes is the first open CDU specification ever contributed to the Open Compute Project. It's Google's fifth-generation design, contributed so that any vendor can build to it.

Second, it's engineered specifically for the next wave of AI and machine learning workloads — two megawatts of thermal capacity at a three-degree-Celsius approach temperature, with eighty to ninety pounds per square inch of usable differential pressure on the IT loop.

Third, the CDU is the precise control point between the Facility Water System and the Technology Cooling System. Inside the unit, parallel heat exchangers transfer heat, redundant pumps drive the IT loop, and a primary-side control valve modulates facility flow to hold the approach temperature stable.

Fourth, every number you'll hear for the rest of the course comes directly from the version one dot zero specification dated February twenty-sixth, twenty twenty-six. If something doesn't match, the document is the source of truth.
